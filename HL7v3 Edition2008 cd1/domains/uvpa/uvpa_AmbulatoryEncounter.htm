Introduction

This topic covers messages about Ambulatory Encounters in the Intent (appointment) mood and the Event mood. In an ambulatory encounter a patient visits a healthcare facility for diagnosis or treatment not requiring an overnight stay. However, to support use cases such as ambulatory surgery and observation patients, the Ambulatory Encounter information model includes an optional Accommodation associated with the patient's assigned location. In HL7 version 2.x terms, an ambulatory encounter is a visit with a Patient Class (PV1-2) of "outpatient".

Scope

Appointment Notifications

This release supports all four unsolicited notification messages defined in the Scheduling domain. The message content is defined at:

  • Appointment New Notification (see Ambulatory Encounter Intent Activate))

  • Appointment Revise Notification (see Ambulatory Encounter Intent Revise)

  • Appointment Cancel Notification (see Ambulatory Encounter Intent Abort)

  • Appointment Reschedule Notification (see Ambulatory Encounter Intent Revise Reschedule)

Event Notifications

This release supports five event notifications. The message content is defined at:

  • Begin Ambulatory Encounter (see Ambulatory Encounter Event Activate)

  • Revise Ambulatory Encounter (see Ambulatory Encounter Event Revise)

  • End Ambulatory Encounter (see Ambulatory Encounter Event Complete)

  • Abort Ambulatory Encounter (see Ambulatory Encounter Event Abort)

  • Nullify Ambulatory Encounter (see Ambulatory Encounter Event Nullify)

Scope Restrictions

The Patient Administration Technical Committee chose to limit scope in this first release by:

  • Limiting storyboards to the simplest cases

  • Excluding order/request messages

  • Excluding notification messages for state transitions "reactivate" and to/from "suspended" (see the Act State Diagram for Patient Administration Messages below; the unsupported states and transitions are grayed out in the diagram)



Act State Diagram for Patient Encounter Messages

The Patient Administration Technical Committee invites implementers with additional requirements to submit content proposals for future releases of the standard.

6.1.1Ambulatory Encounter  (PRPA_ST401001UV02)

Purpose

This storyboard demonstrates the basic flow of an ambulatory encounter from scheduled, through active, to completed.

Exceptional events are described in other storyboards.

  • Canceling a scheduled ambulatory encounter is covered in Cancel Ambulatory Encounter Appointment.

  • Abnormal termination of an active ambulatory encounter is covered in Abort Ambulatory Encounter.

  • Nullifying an erroneously reported ambulatory encounter is covered in Nullify Ambulatory Encounter.

 6.1.1.1Ambulatory Encounter Basic Flow (PRPA_SN401001UV02)

Schedule Ambulatory Encounter

Mr. Adam Everyman is a 64-year-old male with severe chronic pain in his right hip. He called his primary care provider, Dr. Patricia Primary, for consultation. Dr. Primary's receptionist pulled Mr. Everyman's data up on the screen and created an appointment for the next afternoon at 3:00 PM [Trigger Event Schedule Ambulatory Encounter Appointment].

Reschedule Ambulatory Encounter

Before his appointment, Dr. Patricia Primary's receptionist called to tell him that the appointment time needed to be changed. The new appointment was scheduled for 5:00 PM instead of 3:00 PM [Trigger Event Reschedule Ambulatory Encounter Appointment].

Check In

Ten minutes before his scheduled appointment, Mr. Everyman arrived at Dr. Primary's office and checked in with the receptionist. After he reviewed his contact and insurance information he was shown to an examination room [Trigger Event Begin Ambulatory Encounter].

Revise Active Ambulatory Encounter

Dr. Primary examined Mr. Everyman's inflamed hip. She saw that the encounter form incorrectly listed the reason for visit as "pain in left hip" instead of the right hip so she made a correction on the form. She gave Mr. Everyman a prescription for Naprosyn 500 mg. and told him that he did not need to schedule another visit.

Mr. Everyman returned to the front desk and gave the corrected encounter form to the receptionist who corrected the reason for visit [Trigger Event Revise Ambulatory Encounter].

Check Out

The receptionist completed the check out process for Mr. Everyman [Trigger Event End Ambulatory Encounter].

Post Check Out

During the office visit with Dr. Primary, Mr. Everyman mentioned that he did not want his family to worry about his condition. He asked Dr. Primary to be sure not to mention it when he and his wife came in for their next scheduled blood pressure check. When reviewing Mr. Everyman's record later that day, Dr. Primary noticed that the confidentiality request had not been recorded. Mr. Everyman's record was updated to note that the hip condition should not be mentioned to Mr. Everyman's family [Trigger Event Revise Ambulatory Encounter].

6.3.5Begin Ambulatory Encounter  (PRPA_TE401001UV02)
Description
Structured Name:  Ambulatory Encounter Event Activate Notification
Type:  State-transition based
State Transition:  EncounterEvent (PRPA_RM401001UV02)

The Begin Ambulatory Encounter trigger event signals that a new ambulatory encounter has started. This is variously called outpatient registration, ambulatory encounter check-in or admission for a short stay ambulatory encounter. If there was an appointment for this encounter then this trigger event also implicitly changes the status of the appointment to completed.


V2 Reference

The Begin Ambulatory Encounter trigger event is aligned with two HL7 2.4 events, the ADT^A04 - register a patient event signaling the start of an outpatient encounter and the ADT^A01 - admit/visit notification where the patient class in the PV1-2 is outpatient signaling the start of a short stay (less than 24 hours) admission. Note that in v3 a separate trigger event is required to activate a new patient record.

Description
Structured Name:  Ambulatory Encounter Event Revise Notification
Type:  State-transition based
State Transition:  EncounterEvent (PRPA_RM401002UV02)

The Revise Ambulatory Encounter trigger event signals that information about an ambulatory encounter has changed.


V2 Reference

The Revise Ambulatory Encounter trigger event is most closely aligned with the HL7 2.4 ADT^A08 - update patient information event where the patient class in the PV1-2 is outpatient. However, in v3 this trigger event only signals a change to encounter information, changes to a patient's demographic information are signaled by a Revise Patient Information trigger event.

6.4.1Ambulatory Encounter Intent Activate  (PRPA_RM411001UV02)

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   Patient Administration (PRPA_DM000000UV)

The Ambulatory Encounter Intent Activate R-MIM defines the message used to report that a new ambulatory encounter appointment was scheduled.

Encounter Attributes

The focal class (and entry point) to the Ambulatory Encounter Intent Activate R-MIM is the EncounterAppointment class. The attributes of encounter appointment are:

  • id - the unique identifier for this encounter appointment

  • code = AMB, says that is an appointment for an ambulatory encounter

  • statusCode = "active"

  • effectiveTime - the anticipated start and (optional) end time for the scheduled encounter

  • priorityCode - the admission urgency for the scheduled encounter

  • confidentialityCode - a code that controls the disclosure of information about this scheduled patient encounter

  • reasonCode - gives an explanation for the encounter appointment, other than the medical reason for the encounter in the pertinentInformation (diagnoses) described below. Examples of values here are "Medical Necessity", "Patient's Request" and "Dependency".

  • admissionReferralSourceCode - a code used to categorize the type of place or organization responsible for the patient immediately prior to their scheduled encounter; for example, in the United States, this is identified in UB-92 Form Locator 20, Source of Adm(ission). Detailed information about the admitted-from place or type of place is conveyed through the arrivedBy act relationship to the TransportationAppointment (see below).

  • lengthOfStayQuantity - expresses the expected quantity of time for the scheduled encounter

  • acuityLevelCode - a code depicting the anticipated acuity (complexity of patient care, resource intensiveness of the patient care) of a patient's medical condition upon arrival.

  • preAdmitTestInd - indicates whether tests or other preparations were scheduled in a preadmission encounter prior to this scheduled encounter. The actual appointment for the pre-admit tests can be related to this scheduled encounter with the reason act relationship (see below).

  • specialCourtesiesCode - a code identifying special courtesies to be extended to the patient (e.g., no courtesies, extended courtesies, professional courtesy, VIP courtesies).

  • specialArrangementCode - a code indicating the type of special arrangements to be provided for a patient encounter (e.g., wheelchair, stretcher, interpreter, attendant, seeing eye dog). This is not related to the AccommodationEvent.

Links to Other Encounter Appointments

An encounter appointment can be the reason for another encounter appointment for tests or other preparation. The inpatient or ambulatory encounter appointment (target) is the reason for the pre-admission encounter appointment (source). Context conduction ensures that both appointments have the same subject (patient).

Links to Other Acts

Encounter appointments can link to other acts:

  • AccommodationAppointment: the patient's assigned location at the beginning of the encounter, ServiceDeliveryLocation, may be linked to one or more AccommodationAppointment acts via a locationOf participation. Generally an ambulatory encounter would not include an AccommodationAppointment but it is included to support use cases such as ambulatory surgery and observation patients.

  • TransportationAppointment: the patient's scheduled arrival is linked to the encounter via an arrivedBy act relationship. The patient's mode of arrival can be sent as TransportationAppointment.text, the admitted-from location in a R_LocationLocatedEntity CMET, and the time the patient is expected to arrive at the encounter site as the end of the TransportationAppointment.effectiveTime range. Context conduction ensures that the encounter appointment and the transportation appointment have the same subject (patient).

  • A_Account: the scheduled encounter can be linked to one or more patient accounts. Each account is sent in a separate A_Account CMET.

  • A_ObservationDx: the scheduled encounter can be linked to one or more admission diagnoses . Each diagnosis is sent in a separate A_ObservationDx CMET. Multiple admission diagnoses can be rank ordered using the pertinentInformation.priorityNumber.

  • A_Consent: the scheduled encounter can be linked to one or more consents granted by the patient or the patient's representative. Each consent is sent in a separate A_Consent CMET. Note, this s currently a placeholder CMET until the Medical Records TC completes work on medico-legal document modeling.

Direct Participations in the Scheduled Encounter

A number of entities play roles that participate directly in the scheduled encounter:

  • admitter: the healthcare practitioner that required/authorized the scheduled encounter

  • responsibleParty: a healthcare provider organization that will hold clinical responsibility for the patient at the start of the scheduled encounter

  • location: the patient's assigned location at the start of the scheduled encounter

  • subject: the patient who is the subject of the scheduled encounter

  • referrer: the healthcare practitioner who requested the scheduled encounter to take place

  • consultant: an advisor who will participate in the scheduled encounter by performing evaluations and making recommendations

  • attender: the healthcare practitioner who will be primarily responsible for the patient at the start of the scheduled encounter

  • notificationContact: the patient's designated Emergency Contact for this scheduled encounter.

6.4.3Ambulatory Encounter Intent Revise Reschedule  (PRPA_RM411004UV02)

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   Patient Administration (PRPA_DM000000UV)

The Ambulatory Encounter Intent Revise Reschedule R-MIM defines the message used to report that an ambulatory encounter appointment was rescheduled before the encounter began. The composite message changes the date-time of the scheduled encounter, allows changing the patient's initial assigned location and associated AccommodationAppointment, allows changing the details of the patient's arrivedBy planned transportation to the encounter, and conveys the reason the appointment was rescheduled.

ControlActProcess

The reason the appointment was rescheduled can be conveyed in the Trigger Event Control Act portion of the HL7 composite message. The reasonCode attribute of the ControlActProcess conveys a simple, coded reason. More comprehensive information about why the appointment was rescheduled may be conveyed in a A_DetectedIssue CMET associated with the ControlActProcess via a reasonOf act relationship.

6.4.4Ambulatory Encounter Intent Abort  (PRPA_RM411003UV02)

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   Patient Administration (PRPA_DM000000UV)

The Ambulatory Encounter Intent Abort R-MIM defines the message used to report that an ambulatory encounter appointment was canceled before the encounter began. The composite message aborts the encounter appointment, completes managed participations in the appointment, and conveys the reason the appointment was canceled.

ControlActProcess

The reason the appointment was canceled is conveyed in the Trigger Event Control Act portion of the HL7 composite message. The reasonCode attribute of the ControlActProcess conveys a simple, coded reason. More comprehensive information about why the appointment was canceled may be conveyed in a A_DetectedIssue CMET associated with the ControlActProcess via a reasonOf act relationship.

EncounterAppointment

The focal class (and entry point) of the Ambulatory Encounter Intent Abort R-MIM is the EncounterAppointment class. The attributes of encounter are:

  • id - the unique identifier for this encounter appointment

  • code = AMB, says that this was an appointment for an ambulatory encounter

  • statusCode = "aborted"

  • reasonCode - gives an explanation for the encounter, other than the medical reason for the encounter. Examples of values here are "Medical Necessity", "Patient's Request" and "Dependency".

  • lengthOfStayQuantity - expresses the expected quantity of time for the scheduled encounter.

Managed Participations in EncounterAppointment

  • responsibleParty: the healthcare provider organization that was scheduled to hold clinical responsibility for care of the patient at the start of the scheduled encounter. An encounter abort completes the responsibleParty participation.

  • location: the patient's initial assigned location for the scheduled encounter. The location may have also linked the scheduled encounter to an AccommodationAppointment. An encounter abort completes the location participation and scheduled AccommodationAppointment.

  • attender: the healthcare practitioner who was scheduled to be primarily responsible for the patient at the start of the scheduled encounter. An encounter abort completes the attender participation.

Description
Parent:   Patient Administration (PRPA_DM000000UV)

The Ambulatory Encounter Event Active R-MIM defines the message used to report that a new ambulatory encounter has started.

Encounter Attributes

The focal class (and entry point) of the Ambulatory Encounter Event Activate R-MIM is the EncounterEvent class. The attributes of encounter are:

  • id - one or more unique identifiers for this encounter (for example, an "internal" id used only by computer systems and an "external" id for display to users)

  • code = AMB, says that this is an ambulatory encounter

  • statusCode = "active"

  • effectiveTime - a starting time representing the administrative onset of the encounter (e.g. admission, registration, patient arrival) and, optionally, an ending time representing the anticipated date-time of the patient's departure (e.g. discharge). All messages, except the "nullify" message, require at least a starting time.

  • activityTime - the total time interval for an encounter including preparation before the patient arrives and clean-up actions after the patient departs. This would probably be null in an encounter activate message.

  • priorityCode - the encounter admission urgency.

  • confidentialityCode - a code that controls the disclosure of information about this patient encounter.

  • reasonCode - gives an explanation for the encounter, other than the medical reason for the encounter in the pertinentInformation1 (diagnoses) described below. Examples of values here are "Medical Necessity", "Patient's Request" and "Dependency".

  • admissionReferralSourceCode - a code used to categorize the type of place or organization responsible for the patient immediately prior to their admission. In the US realm this references the UB92 FL 20 - Source of Admission value set.

  • lengthOfStayQuantity - expresses the expected quantity of time for the encounter.

  • dischargeDispositionCode - A code depicting the expected disposition of the patient at the time of discharge. In the US realm this references the UB92 FL 22 - Patient Status value set.

  • acuityLevelCode - a code depicting the acuity (complexity of patient care, resource intensiveness of the patient care) of a patient's medical condition upon arrival.

  • preAdmitTestInd - indicates whether tests or other preparations were done in a preadmission encounter prior to this admission. The actual encounter for the pre-admit tests can be related to the encounter with the reason act relationship (see below).

  • specialCourtesiesCode - a code identifying special courtesies extended to the patient (e.g., no courtesies, extended courtesies, professional courtesy, VIP courtesies).

  • specialArrangementCode - a code indicating the type of special arrangements provided for a patient encounter (e.g., wheelchair, stretcher, interpreter, attendant, seeing eye dog). This is not related to the AccommodationEvent.

Links to Other Encounters

Encounters can relate to other encounters in three ways:

  • reason: this can link the encounter to one or more pre-admission ambulatory encounters for tests or other preparation. The inpatient or ambulatory encounter (target) is the reason for the pre-admission encounter (source). Context conduction ensures that all encounters have the same subject (patient). Note that the Encounter.preAdmitTestInd should also be set to "true".

  • sequelTo: this can link the encounter to a previous encounter for the same patient. Examples include: (1) a patient in an ambulatory encounter is admitted as an inpatient after an evaluation of the seriousness of the patient's condition, (2) an inpatient is discharged from an inpatient encounter directly to an ambulatory encounter as part of the same episode of care, (3) a patient is re-admitted to a healthcare facility. Context conduction ensures that both encounters have the same subject (patient).

  • componentOf: this can link the encounter to one or more sub-encounters performed within the main encounter. Examples include: (1) an outpatient visit includes clinical encounters with radiology and clinical specialists, (2) an emergency room visit includes encounters for two non-related issues (blurry vision in the eye and a sprained knee, causing the patient to be sent to an eye specialist and an orthopedist). Context conduction ensures that all encounters have the same subject (patient)

Links to Other Acts

Encounters can link to other acts:

  • AccommodationEvent: the patient's assigned location during an encounter, ServiceDeliveryLocation, can be linked to one or more AccommodationEvent acts via a locationOf participation. Generally an ambulatory encounter would not include an AccommodationEvent but it is included to support use cases such as ambulatory surgery and observation patients.

  • PatientTransportation: the patient's arrival can be linked to the encounter via an arrivedBy act relationship. The patient's mode of arrival can be sent as PatientTransportation.text, the admitted-from location in a R_LocationLocatedEntity CMET, and the time patient arrived at the encounter site as the end of thePatientTransportation.effectiveTime range. Both the performer (person who transported the patient) and an escort can be sent in R_AssignedPerson [contact] CMETs. Context conduction ensures that the encounter and the transportation have the same subject (patient).

  • A_Appointment: the encounter can be linked to the appointment that scheduled the encounter. The scheduled encounter and the actual encounter should have the same value for type in Encounter.code. Context conduction ensures that the appointment and the encounter have the same subject (patient). Activation of the encounter event implicitly changes the status code of the appointment to completed.

  • A_Account: the encounter can be linked to one or more patient accounts. Each account is sent in a separate A_Account CMET.

  • A_ObservationDx: the encounter can be linked to one or more diagnoses. Each diagnosis is sent in a separate A_ObservationDx CMET. The encounter "activate" interaction would send an admission diagnosis (Observation.code of "ADMX" in the A_ObservationDx CMET). Multiple admission diagnoses can be rank ordered using the pertinentInformation1.priorityNumber.

  • A_Consent: the encounter can be linked to one or more consents granted by the patient or patient's representative. Each consent is sent in a separate A_Consent CMET. Note, this is currently a placeholder CMET until the Medical Records TC completes work on medico-legal document modeling.

  • ValuablesLocation: the encounter can be linked to one or more reports of patient valuables stored during the encounter. Each report is sent as a free text observation of the valuables and where they are stored during the encounter. If no valuables were stored for the patient then an ValuablesLocation can be sent with a negationInd of "true".

Direct Participations in the Encounter

A number of entities play roles that participate directly in the encounter:

  • admitter: the healthcare practitioner that required/authorized the encounter, usually in the case of an inpatient encounter. Note: the admitter here must be the same as the author reported in the HL7 Trigger Event Control Act for the admission.

  • responsibleParty: the healthcare provider organization that holds clinical responsibility for care of the patient during the encounter. The encounter "activate" interaction activates the initial responsibleParty participation.

  • location: the patient's assigned location during the encounter. The encounter "activate" interaction activates the initial location participation. ServiceDeliveryLocation is a role played by a place at which services may be provided. A service delivery location may be either an incidental service delivery location (a place at which services may be provided without prior designation or authorization) or a dedicated service delivery location (a place that is intended to house the provision of services). Dedicated service delivery locations can be further characterized as either clinical (DedicatedClinicalLocationRoleType) or non-clinical (DedicatedNonClinicalLocationRoleType).

  • subject: the patient who is the subject of the encounter.

  • referrer: the healthcare practitioner who requested the encounter to take place.

  • consultant: an advisor participating in the encounter by performing evaluations and making recommendations.

  • attender: the healthcare practitioner who is primarily responsible for the patient during a period in time in the given encounter. The encounter "activate" interaction activates the initial attender participation.

  • notificationContact: the patient's designated Emergency Contact for this encounter.

Description
Parent:   Patient Administration (PRPA_DM000000UV)

The Ambulatory Encounter Event Revise R-MIM defines the message used to report that information about an ambulatory encounter has changed. The ambulatory encounter can be either active or completed.

Differences

Ambulatory Encounter Event Revise R-MIM differs from the Ambulatory Encounter Event Activate R-MIM in the following ways:

  • EncounterEvent does not include the statusCode attribute because state changes in EncounterEvent are conveyed by the trigger event identified in the Trigger Event Control Act.

  • responsibleParty - the encounter revise message includes the current responsibleParty but should not be used to change the active responsibleParty. This is done with a message derived from the Encounter Organization Change R-MIM.

  • discharger - the encounter revise message includes an optional discharger participation in EncounterEvent. The discharger is the healthcare practitioner that required/authorized the ending of an encounter.

  • location - the encounter revise message includes the current location but should not be used to change the active location. This is done with a message derived from the Encounter Location Change R-MIM.

  • The single arrivedBy act relationship from EncounterEvent to PatientTransportation is replaced with up to two transportedBy act relationships permitting both patient arrival and patient departure transportation events.

  • attender - the encounter revise message includes the current attender but should not be used to change the active attender. This is done with a message derived from the Attending Practitioner Change R-MIM.

6.4.7Ambulatory Encounter Event Abort  (PRPA_RM401004UV02)

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   Patient Administration (PRPA_DM000000UV)

The Ambulatory Encounter Event Abort R-MIM defines the message used to report that an ambulatory encounter was aborted prior to normal completion. The composite message aborts the encounter, completes the managed participations in the encounter, and conveys the reason the encounter was aborted.

ControlActProcess

The reason the encounter was aborted is conveyed in the Trigger Event Control Act portion of the HL7 composite message. The reasonCode attribute of the ControlActProcess conveys a simple, coded reason. More comprehensive information about why the encounter was aborted may be conveyed in a A_DetectedIssue CMET associated with the ControlActProcess via a reasonOf act relationship.

EncounterEvent

The focal class (and entry point) of the Ambulatory Encounter Event Abort R-MIM is the EncounterEvent class. The attributes of encounter are:

  • id - one or more unique identifiers for this encounter (for example, an "internal" id used only by computer systems and an "external" id for display to users)

  • code = AMB, says that this is an ambulatory encounter

  • statusCode = "aborted"

  • effectiveTime - the time interval starting with the administrative onset of the encounter (e.g. admission, registration, patient arrival) and ending with the patient's departure (e.g. discharge).

  • activityTime - the total time interval for an encounter including preparation before the patient arrives and clean-up actions after the patient departs.

  • reasonCode - gives an explanation for the encounter, other than the medical reason. Examples of values here are "Medical Necessity", "Patient's Request" and "Dependency".

  • lengthOfStayQuantity - expresses the actual quantity of time for the encounter.

  • dischargeDispositionCode - A code depicting the disposition of the patient at the time of discharge (e.g., discharged to home, expired, against medical advice, etc.). In the US realm this references the UB92 FL 22 - Patient Status value set.

EncounterEvent Links to Other Acts

Encounters can link to other acts:

  • AccommodationEvent: the patient's assigned location during an encounter, ServiceDeliveryLocation, can be linked to one or more AccommodationEvent acts via a locationOf participation. Generally an ambulatory encounter would not include an AccommodationEvent but it is included to support use cases such as ambulatory surgery and observation patients.

  • PatientTransportation: the patient's departure can be linked to the encounter act via a departedBy act relationship. The patient's mode of departure can be sent as PatientTransportation.text, the discharged-to location in a R_LocationLocatedEntity CMET, and the time patient arrived (or is anticipated to arrive) at the discharged-to site as the end of thePatientTransportation.effectiveTime range. Both the performer (person who transported the patient) and an escort can be sent in R_AssignedPerson [contact] CMETs. Context conduction ensures that the encounter and the transportation have the same subject (patient).

Direct Participations in EncounterEvent

A number of entities play roles that participate directly in the encounter:

  • responsibleParty: the healthcare provider organization that holds clinical responsibility for care of the patient during the encounter. An encounter abort completes the current responsibleParty participation.

  • location: the patient's assigned location during the encounter. An encounter abort completes the current location participation.

  • subject: the patient who is the subject of the encounter.

  • attender: the healthcare practitioner who is primarily responsible for the patient during a period in time in the given encounter. An encounter abort completes the current attender participation.

  • notificationContact: the patient's designated Emergency Contact for this encounter in case they need to be contacted about the unexpected end of the encounter.

6.4.8Ambulatory Encounter Event Complete  (PRPA_RM401003UV02)

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   Patient Administration (PRPA_DM000000UV)

The Ambulatory Encounter Event Complete R-MIM defines the message used to report that an ambulatory encounter has ended normally

Differences

Ambulatory Encounter Event Complete R-MIM differs from the Ambulatory Encounter Event Activate R-MIM in the following ways:

  • The EncounterEvent.statusCode is set to "completed".

  • The EncounterEvent.effectiveTime contains actual values for both start and end date-times instead of an anticipated end date-time.

  • The EncounterEvent.activityTime is more likely to contain a value.

  • The EncounterEvent.lengthOfStayQuantity, if valued, contains the actual, calculated quantity (the actual days quantity cannot be simply calculated from the admission and discharge dates because of possible leaves of absence) instead of the expected length of stay.

  • The EncounterEvent.dischargeDispositionCode, if valued, contains the actual discharge disposition instead of the expected discharge disposition.

  • The departedBy act relationship from EncounterEvent to PatientTransportation replaces the arrivedBy act relationship. The patient's mode of departure can be sent as PatientTransportation.text, the discharged-to location in a R_LocationLocatedEntity CMET, and the time the patient departed from the encounter site as the start of thePatientTransportation.effectiveTime range and time the patient arrived (or is expected to arrive) at the discharged-to location as the end of the time range. Both the performer (person who transported the patient) and an escort can be sent in R_AssignedPerson [contact] CMETs. Context conduction ensures that the encounter and the transportation have the same subject (patient).

  • There is an optional discharger participation in EncounterEvent. The discharger is the healthcare practitioner that required/authorized the ending of an encounter.
