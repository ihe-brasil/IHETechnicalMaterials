 5.1Introduction
 5.1.1 Individual Case Safety Report Release 2

i - Notes to Readers

The Individual Case Safety Report (ICSR) Release 2 Draft Standard for Trial Use (DSTU) was approved by the Patient Safety Special Interest Group (PSSIG) and endorsed by the Regulated Clinical Research Information Management Technical Committee at the January 2007 Working Group Meeting. PSSIG submitted a formal request to the HL7 Board to add ICSR to the HL7 DSTU Comment Page June 12, 2007. Approval was received from the Board on June 21, 2007. The DSTU will be available for download from the HL7 DSTU webpage August 1, 2007 through July 1, 2008.

PSSIG will collaborate with the HL7 Implementation and Conformance Technical Committee to develop test plans, tools and other documentation to support ICSR DSTU testing. The US Food and Drug Administration will serve as an early adopter organization, and will coordinate with other parties interested in testing or further development of the standard. Interested parties should contact Ms. Lise Stevens, FDA at (301) 827-6085 or via email at Lise.Stevens@fda.hhs.gov.

iii - Changes from Previous Release

The Individual Case Safety Report (ICSR) Release 2 expands the scope of the 2005 Normative ICSR Release 1 standard to support other product categories such as food, food additives, dietary supplements, cosmetics, veterinary drug products, and combination products. Additional changes to the specification have been made to accommodate new requirements based upon the experience of pilot testing ICSR Release 1 to support electronic medical device reporting for the FDA Center for Devices and Radiological Health (CDRH), and support for human and animal reporting. A more detailed list of other improvements can be found in the Introduction section of the ICSR DSTU material.

iv - Known Issues and Planned Changes

PSSIG has not finalized logistics for posting material and will continue to coordinate with HL7 Headquarters and the Implementation and Conformance Technical Committee for guidance. The committee suggests that interested parties join the PSSIG list server, where documents and progress notes will be posted. The ICSR DSTU is available for download and comments can be posted to the HL7 DSTU Comment webpage: http://www.hl7.org/dstucomments/index.cfm

In addition to improvements introduced as a result of FDA DSTU testing, the committee is aware of ongoing work in the International Standards Organization (ISO) Technical Committee 215 concerning the collaboration between ISO, the European Committee on Standardization (CEN), the International Conference on Harmonisation (ICH) and HL7 to create an international adverse event reporting standard. This work is expected to support ICH requirements for revision to their E2B(M) Message Specification. The committee anticipates this work to be presented to HL7 sometime next year, and will incorporate new requirements into the next release of the standard.

5.3.1ICSR Refined Message Information Model  (PORR_RM049006UV01)

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   Public Health Reporting Domain Information Model (PORR_DM000000UV)

Model Overview

The Individual Case Safety Report (ICSR) Refined Message Information Models (RMIMs) capture information about adverse events and product problems that are reported to public health, patient safety/quality improvement organizations or regulatory agencies. The ICSR message supports reporting from a variety of reporting sources such as consumers, hospitals, contract research organizations, clinicians or pharmaceutical product and medical device manufacturers. The HL7 ICSR Release 1 specification was most particularly oriented towards reporting of reactions to regulated drugs, therapeutic biologics, vaccines, and medical devices. In addition to the products supported in release 1, the ICSR Release 2 supports a wider variety of products including: a) veterinary products, b) food and food additives, c) dietary supplements and cosmetics, and d) combination products. ICSR Release 2 provides a minimal level of backward compatibility or harmonization with ICSR Release 1, by supporting concepts and attributes introduced in the original release, including international pharmacovigilence reporting requirements described in the International Conference on Harmonisation's (ICH) E2B M2 ICSR message transmission specification.

Implementation efforts by the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) for ICSR Release 1 to support medical device reporting revealed several limitations to the standard which required substantial changes to the ICSR Release 1 RMIMs. Some examples of these changes include streamlining the schema to support the reporting of adverse events and product problems within a single instance of the message, and the addition of a device observation class that is used to capture results from product evaluations performed by medical device manufacturers or reprocessors. Additionally, new requirements for other FDA regulated products required the creation of generalized product and product use models to capture concepts and attributes that can be used to support combination product reporting. The new R_Product RMIM introduced in ICSR Release 2 is based upon the Release 1 product-specific RMIMs for drugs (R_Drug) and devices (R_Device). These RMIMs are included in Release 2 to maintain a minimal level of backward compatibility between ICSR Release and 1 and Release 2; however, the ICSR Release 1 attributes for both drugs and devices are accommodated in the new generic R_Product RMIM.

The RMIMs are oriented around the following concepts:

a) Adverse experiences: Any adverse event associated with the use of a product in humans or animals, whether or not considered product related, including the following: An adverse event occurring in the course of the use of a product in professional practice; an adverse event occurring from overdose of the product whether accidental or intentional; an adverse event occurring from abuse of the product; an adverse event occurring from withdrawal of the product; and any failure of expected pharmacological action. Note these events may result in unintended harm to the patient by an act of commission or omission, rather than by the underlying disease or condition of the patient.

b) Suspected adverse reaction: A noxious and unintended response to a product for which there is a reasonable possibility that the product caused the response. In this definition, the phrase "a reasonable possibility" means that the relationship cannot be ruled out. The range of product types for which reports may be generated is set by regulatory and/or organizational administrative practice within the jurisdiction controlling the reporting between parties.

c) Suspected product problems or defects: Any problem or defect observed or detected before a product is used. This includes the use of drugs, biologics or medical devices used for treatment, or other consumer products such as cosmetics or dietary supplements. Some examples of product problems include faulty packaging or labeling, receipt of an expired therapeutic drug product, or suspected contamination based upon inappropriate or unauthorized preparations or mixtures.

d) An affected person or animal: If applicable to the report, this includes the investigative subject (patient) that experienced an adverse event.

e) Substance administrations: Includes information about how the product was given or used, or intended to be given or used by the investigative subject.

f) Medical procedures related to devices: Includes information about how a medical device was used or intended to be used by a patient or investigative subject. For example, device implant or removal procedures.

g) Supporting clinical information: Includes additional clinical detail such as relevant observations, procedures, substance administrations, supply acts or patient encounters. These observations are included if the reporter considers them relevant to the investigation, whether referring to the same point in time as the suspect event or as part of the patient's medical history.

The ICSR RMIM is designed to support within a single structure two aspects of product reporting. These include: 1) a report about the investigation into the adverse event(s) or reaction(s) suffered by an affected person or animal that experienced an intervention or interventions in a therapeutic context. The suspect event may or may not have a causal relationship with the administration or use of one or more products; and 2) a report about the investigation into a product problem. Note that the key common factor is the involvement of an implicated product such as a medication, device, dietary supplement or cosmetic.

This message, as with all HL7 V3 specifications, includes a Control Act structure which contains information about the actual report transmission. (The reader should refer to the HL7 V3 Infrastructure Management Domain for more details on this structure.)

5.3.2ICSR A_DrugIntervention  (PORR_RM049007UV01)

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   Public Health Reporting Domain Information Model (PORR_DM000000UV)

Model Overview

The A_DrugIntervention message element type captures relevant substance administration act information for cases in which use of a drug is associated with to a reported adverse reaction. The relevant information includes items related to the substance administration itself, to component parts of a more general administration act, as well as to ancillary information related to the substance administration. This message element type is being used to capture substance administrations for drugs, therapeutic biologics, and vaccines. In the ICSR Base model RMIM, this information is associated with the InvestigationEvent class through the ImplicatedAct association.

Note to implementers: This RMIM should only be used by implementers who are interested in drug adverse events and for whom compatibility with the HL7 Structured Product Labeling (SPL) model is of key importance. Otherwise, the committee recommends the use of the A_ProductUse RMIM.

Model Overview

The R_Drug message element type captures the drug product information that is relevant for related adverse event and product problem reporting. The term "drug" includes drugs, therapeutic biologics, and vaccines. Note that the R_Product message element type can also be used to capture drug product information. The relevant information includes items related to the drug itself and information about its manufacture and approval. The reader should note that this model has been modified to follow the pattern of the Structured Product Label (SPL) medication information model created by the Regulated Clinical Information Management Technical Committee the. The model was modified to minimize compatibility concerns for organizations which have already implemented SPL and intend to also use ICSR in the near future.

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   Public Health Reporting Domain Information Model (PORR_DM000000UV)

Model Overview

The R_Device message element type captures specific medical device information that is relevant for device related adverse event and product problem reports. Note that early adopters of ICSR Release 1 should use this model and contact the FDA Center for Devices and Radiological Health (CDRH) to obtain more information about medical device reporting. The new ICSR Release 2 R_Product model can also be used for new implementations to capture device product information. The relevant information includes items related to the device itself, its manufacture, and evaluations performed by device manufacturers or reprocessors.

A device in this context refers to a manufactured product that is used directly in treating and/or caring for the patient. The model captures information about devices by using one central class - Device - to capture descriptive information directly related to the particular device, and another - DeviceModel to capture information about the specific type of device.

5.3.6ICSR R_Product  (PORR_RM049011UV01)

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   Public Health Reporting Domain Information Model (PORR_DM000000UV)

Model Overview

The R_Product message element type captures product information relevant for a wide range of adverse event and product problem reporting requirements. This information includes items related to the product itself, its packaging, manufacture and potential post-event evaluation by manufacturers or reprocessors. Note that information about how a product was supplied (ordered) can be captured using the relevant Supply Act in the Supporting Clinical Statement CMET.

A product in this context refers to a manufactured or home fabricated item that is used directly by an investigative subject - whether person or animal. This model serves three purposes:

  • It provides a generic view of a product as opposed to the more specific product models for medicines (R_Drug) and medical devices (R_Device). This structure supports the FDA's (and presumably other reporting authorities') need for capturing product problems and adverse events associated with the use of other products such as foods, food additives, cosmetics, dietary supplements and veterinary drug products. The new structure provides all the attributes and functionality of the more specific product models.
  • It addresses the need for reporting related to combination products. This category includes products which combine drugs, biologics and/or medical devices into one product for therapeutic use. It also includes situations in which two or more separately licensed products are used together to achieve a specific purpose as documented in the relevant labeling information.
  • It provides a consolidated structure that can be used to replace the more specific product models for medicines and medical devices. This new structure supports migration for unification of the messaging specifications that support a wider variety of product types

Please refer to the HMD documentation for more detailed information on classes and attributes.

5.3.7ICSR A_ProductUse  (PORR_RM049012UV01)

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   Public Health Reporting Domain Information Model (PORR_DM000000UV)

Model Overview

The A_ProductUse message element type captures information about the acts that establish how a product was used by an investigative subject for product related adverse events and/or product problem (defect) reporting. The information includes items relevant to the use of the product and any associated clinical or other information directly related to the product's use. Note that this RMIM supports all attributes and functionality provided in the ICSR Release 1 A_DrugIntervention and A_DeviceIntervention models. ICSR Release 2 retained the use of these models to support a minimum level of backward compatibility between ICSR Release 1 and Release 2. However, for organizations that have not previously implemented ICSR Release 1, the committee recommends use of A_ProductUse, and caution implementers that the ICSR Release 1 intervention models may be deprecated over time in future releases of ICSR.

The ProductUse This choice structure captures information directly related to the use of the product that is relevant to the investigation. If it is necessary to introduce component usage, such as substance administrations or procedures, this initial usage will provide the overall context for the investigation.

Please refer to the HMD documentation for more detailed information on classes and attributes.
