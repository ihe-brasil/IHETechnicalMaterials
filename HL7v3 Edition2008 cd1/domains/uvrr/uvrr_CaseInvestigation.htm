 3.1.1.1Case Investigation Event (PORR_SN100001UV01)

The Regional Health Department of a particular jurisdiction, has been alerted of a possible case of bacterial meningitis. Consultation with the local health authority established that a 2 1/2 year old girl had presented to hospital H a day earlier with fever, nausea, vomiting, and a petechial rash, which was diagnosed as suspected meningococcal sepsis. The Regional Health Department has also learned that a specimen that was cultured from the girl grew Neisseria Meningitidis confirming the suspected diagnosis.suspected meningococcal sepsis.

Consistent with local practice, an notification of case is sent to the national public health authorities [Interaction: Notifiable Condition Report] (In the US, the notification would go to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention.). The notifiable condition message includes relevant clinical information, as well as some demographic data, but does not include identifying information for the patient.

Description
Parent:   Public Health Reporting Domain Information Model (PORR_DM100001UV)

Model Overview

The Notifiable Condition Refined Message Information Model captures the information needed to support case reporting between different jurisdictional levels within the public health system. This specification includes, most particularly, reporting of cases subject to mandatory reporting by statute. That is to say, it supports reporting of notifiable diseases or conditions. It also supports related reporting such as the messages that would be sent by a field investigation team back to its supporting health department.

It is important to note that an actual message also includes a Control Act structure. That area of the message includes information about the actual report transmission. (The reader should refer to the Infrastructure Management Domain for more details.)

Case Investigation

The Case Investigation serves as the entry point for messaging. For case reporting, a case is typically an incident of disease, whether infectious or not. It could also reflect an incident of another type, such as an automobile or a violent crime that is being tracked for public health purposes.

In the United States, identifying data for affected purposes is commonly removed when cases are reported from a State Health Department to the Federal government (.e.g., Centers for Disease Control and Prevention). There are other circumstances, for example, reporting from a local authority or provider to a State Health Department, or reporting between states in which identifying information is included.

Participations

The following participations are defined for a case. Each participation captures the relationship between the case and an entity or another type of act that plays a significant part in the case. (Note, the participations have been ordered by "walking" around the central case in a clockwise direction.)

  • authorOrPerformer: As is usual within HL7 messaging, there may be information that captures the author and/or performers of an act. The particular information captured for this party is defined within the R_AssignedEntity Common Message Element Type (CMET).
  • responsibleParty: The participation records the controlling jurisdiction, typically a national entity, for the investigation. The particular information captured for this party is defined within the R_AssignedEntity CMET.
  • component(Component Act): The central content of the case report consists of a collection of significant acts related to the case. These acts could be observations, substance administrations, or procedures.
    • Observation: The observations include the relevant clinical or contextual facts that are captured for the case. The attribute, ActCode, plays a key role for these. It identifies the type of act that is being recorded or requested
    • Substance Administration: It is often relevant to capture substance administrations related to a public health case. A common example is the record of vaccinations that have been received by a patient. In some cases, this could include orders for vaccinations to be administered to the patient.
    • Procedure: It is often relevant to capture procedures related to a public health case.
    There are a number of associations that are relevant for component acts. These are discussed below. The reader will note that some of these are only relevant for particular types of component act.
    • authorOrPerformer: This is the party who is responsible for the information recorded for the act. The particular information captured for this party is defined within the R_AssignedEntity CMET.
    • location: This is the location where the act was performed. The particular information captured for this party is defined within the R_LocationServiceDelivery CMET.
    • subject(Specimen): Some observations are generated through direct examination of a specimen, and, when this happens, it is often relevant to collect specimen information. The reader should note that specimen information is captured within the context of the specimen CMET. The features of this CMET also support information about the specimen source and the act of specimen collection.
    • consumable: A substance administration typically includes a consumable participation that makes it possible to capture information about the product administered during the substance administration.
    • recordTarget(CaseSubject): Component acts are most usually associated with the case subject - be it person, non person living thing or place- and in this situation the participation is inherited as part of the investigation context. However the component act may also be related to a contact that has been associated with the case subject. When this happens, the act subject is identified using this participation.
    • sourceOf: This allows the creation of relationships between component acts. For example, Component Observations can have additional observations linked to them. This makes it possible to capture clusters or related observations which, as a whole, are related to the case. It is also possible, and sometimes relevant, to capture participating entities, and the relevant location for an observation. It is possible for component substance administrations or component procedures to have observations linked to them.
  • trigger(NotifiableCondition): The cause of an investigation is the notifiable condition that triggered the investigation of that condition. This, most typically, is a case of infectious disease; however the list of conditions that require notification is determined by the regulations of the controlling jurisdiction.

    An incident of human disease or other person related incident is the predominant trigger for case reporting, and information about the person who has come down with the disease is captured within the subject participation for the notifiable condition. Information for patient encounters related to the notifiable condition may also be captured. The reader should note that many of the component acts associated with the case investigation may have originally been linked with the notifiable condition. However, in the message all the acts are related to the case investigation for the sake of simplicity.

    In particular, the notifiable condition is linked to the case subject. This normally is a person who has become ill. However, in some situations, the subject of investigation will be an animal (non-person living subject) or a location.

  • pertinentInformation(SecondaryCaseNotification): This act provides a way of capturing information for related notifiable condition reports that the report sender feels are relevant to the investigation, and to consideration of this report These reports are listed as secondary notifications, because they are based on the primary case notification - that is on the original report of a case from a health care provider, or other party such as a family member or member of the public.
  • subject(PrimaryContact): It is often the case that the underlying justification for public health's interest in a disease or condition is related to the disease's potential to be communicated to others. (This interest is what lies behind the designation of something as a case.) Such communicability is clearly relevant for infectious diseases; it is also relevant for toxins such as anthrax which can be dispersed from a place to persons, and passed from person to person through casual contact. This participation captures information about persons who play the role of contact with respect to the person, place, or non-person living thing that is the subject of the case (the scoper of the contact role). It is also possible to identify persons - secondary contacts - who come in contact with primary contacts or other secondary contacts.
