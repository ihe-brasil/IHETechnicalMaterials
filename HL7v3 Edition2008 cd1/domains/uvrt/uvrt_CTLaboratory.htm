Introduction

The clinical laboratory messages under development in other domains are triggered by a state change in an individual observation event. They are designed to send immediate information as observations are made.

During the course of clinical trials, however, data are not exchanged on an event-transition basis. Rather, data is held by the sender for a period of time, until an agreed upon criterion has been reached for transmission. That criterion may be time based or event based, but the event will not be a change in an object contained in the message.

Under these circumstances, successive nesting of information in the message represents a significant saving of space over a format that repeats trial, site, investigator, subject, study event and specimen information for each individual observation. This standard is designed to take advantage of those savings, while adopting the Laboratory domain's DMIM for observations.

3.1.1Periodic Reporting of CT Laboratory Results  (PORT_ST030001UV01)

Purpose

The purpose of this storyboard is to illustrate the aspects of the conduct of a clinical trial (including communication of results) which affect the transfer of clinical trial laboratory data from the contract or reference laboratory responsible for the analyses to the trial sponsor or sponsor's agent.

The business case for periodic reporting of laboratory results was first developed by the Clinical Data Interchange Standards Consortium (CDISC) Laboratory Data Team, and further developed in the collaboration between CDISC and HL7. The business data requirements defined by CDISC for this storyboard have been published as CDISC Laboratory Data Interchange Standard Version 1.0 (www.CDISC.org). Those data requirements are presented in the second section below.

 3.1.1.1CTLaboratory Periodic Report to Sponsor/Agent (PORT_SN030001UV01)

Interactions: CTLaboratory Periodic Report to Sponsor/Agent (PORT_IN030001)

A biopharmaceutical company (sponsor) is conducting a clinical trial of a new drug XYZ. The company has hired a central laboratory to perform the specimen testing and a contract research organization (CRO) to collect, clean and compile the study data as the sponsor's agent.

The clinical trial is to be performed at multiple study sites, with each study site having multiple investigators. Each candidate subject is screened for eligibility, and is assigned an identifier solely for this screening process. If the candidate subject is found to qualify to participate in the study, the subject is enrolled and assigned a subject identifier for the study. Throughout the subject's participation (candidate and enrolled), the subject is assigned to one or another investigator at the site at which the subject is participating. Subjects may be reassigned to new sites and/or investigators during the course of the subject's participation in the study.

Specimens are collected from each subject during both the screening and enrolled phases for that subject. These specimens are forwarded to the central laboratory, along with information identifying the subject and the specific point in the study at which the specimen was collected. The central laboratory accessions these specimens. The laboratory also assigns an additional identifier to each subject for use within the laboratory's own processing. The central laboratory may forward some or all of the specimen material to another laboratory for testing.

The laboratory responsible for testing performs the tests specified in the sponsor's protocol for each specimen, and records the results in its own data management system. Data from other than the central laboratory is forwarded to the central laboratory, which is responsible for reporting all results to the sponsor/agent.

At agreed-upon points, the central laboratory reports all results developed since the last reporting point (including updates and deletions) to the CRO (interaction: CTLaboratory Periodic Report to Sponsor/Agent (PORT_IN030001)). As each subject completes the study, and after all anomalies in that subject's data have been resolved, the CRO reports all results for that subject to the sponsor (interaction: CTLaboratory Periodic Report to Sponsor/Agent (PORT_IN030001)).

The CDISC Laboratory Data Interchange Standard Version 1.0 (www.CDISC.org) has been taken as the foundation for the HL7 Version 3 message presented in this domain.

Click here to access the CDISC Standard.

The CDISC standard data requirements are represented in the following diagram:

The rationale for nesting clinical trial results for periodic reporting (as contrasted with immediate reporting of individual observations) are discussed in the Regulated Studies domain Introduction and Scope section above. To achieve nesting in the CDISC laboratory model, the clinical trial is broken down into individual sites, and then into investigators at each site. Subjects are identified for each investigator. The next level represents the study events (often called visits) for a particular subject, and laboratory specimens collected during each study event are accessioned. Diverging branches for representation of observations on different types of specimens (standard/base, microbiologic, genomic, ..) are anticipated, but the current message deals only with the base specimen type. These specimens are subjected to test batteries, which in turn are headers for their respective tests and results. The diagram above lists the data to be represented at each level of nesting. These data are defined in the published CDISC standard.

In addition to nested data, the business model requires the ability to specify transaction type (add, update, delete, no change) at each level of the hierarchy. This business requirement allows the sender to indicate the action the receiver should take relative to specific (not necessarily all) data in the message. The transaction type applies at the level at which it is specified, and to all data below it in the message, unless the transaction type is respecified at that lower level. Such overrides must follow the rules of logic. For example, a delete at the subject level cannot be overridden by an add at the study event level, since there is no subject for which the study event can be added. On the other hand, it is possible to specify an update of the Study Event (eg. new visit name), followed by an unchanged (previously sent) Accession with a newspecimen for that Study Event. Individual transmission agreements may tighten these constraints (eg. to specify that once a transmission type has been set, it cannot be overridden at a lower level of nesting).

The update type transaction cannot change any required fields, since it relies on those fields to identify the object to be updated. In addition, the delete transaction type applies to all data in the receivers posession that is logically below that level in the hierarchy - and which has been received from the sender. Individual transmission agreements can specify whether or not the sender must include all lower-level data to be deleted.

If the delete is for the purpose of changing a required field, all of the lower level data that has been sent previously must be retransmitted as new, following transmission of the new data at the previously deleted level.

The Refined Message Information Model (RMIM) recasts the committee's business model in terms of the HL7 Reference Information Model (RIM). After the information requirements for representing the superstructure of a clinical trial are addressed (protocol, trial, trial site, investigators, subjects, study events), the RMIM uses the Laboratory Observation domain's DMIM as the foundation.

Representation of the Business Model in the RMIM

It will be helpful to open the RMIM and the Business Model to follow along with the discussion of how the RMIM represents the business requirements.

Business Model Category: Study

Study identifiers (id, name) are represented in the id and title attributes of the ClinicalTrial class on the RMIM.

Business Model Category: Study Site

All of the trial activity performed at a specific site is represented in the class TrialAtSite, which is a component of the ClinicalTrial. The physical location of the site is represented by the Site class, which plays the role of TrialSite (the service delivery location) in the StudyAtSite act.

Business Model Category: Investigator

The trial activity performed by a specific investigator at a specific site is represented by the class InvestigatorAtSite, which is a component of TrialAtSite. The individual functioning as investigator is represented by the NamedPerson class, and plays the role of TrialInvestigator in the TrialAtSite act.

Business Model Category: Subject

A particular subject is assigned to a particular investigator (at a site, for a study) through the SubjectAssignment act, which is a component of InvestigatorAtSite. None of the required data is captured in this act, but it serves to tie together the subject, study and visits. The person who is the subject of the study is represented by the Person class, where the initials, sex, birthdate and race can be specified. Codelists are included as components of the attributes capturing this information. This person participates in a particular SubjectAssignment through one of the two subject roles, EnrolledSubject or ScreeningSubject. A third subject role -- SpareSubject -- is available but cannot be the sole identifier for the subject in the study. A choice of identifier is made when a subject participates in a SubjectAssignment. If the EnrolledSubject role is chosen, the ScreeningSubject identifier can be specified as well. If the ScreeningSubject role is chosen, however, the EnrolledSubject identifier cannot be specified. A second SubjectAssignment will advance the subject from screening to enrolled.

Business Model Category: Study Event

Study events are represented by the TimepointEvent class - a component of a study assignment (i.e. as events that happen to a subject assigned to a specific investigator, site and trial). Visit name and number from the business model are captured in the code attribute of the TimepointEvent class; and visit type (scheduled, retest, etc.) in the reasonCode.

Business Model Category: Accession

Accession identifier and last active date are represented in the Accession class. The responsible laboratory is the Laboratory that scopes the Agent who authors the Accession act.

Business Model Category: Base Specimen

The act of BaseSpecimenDefinition links the Accession to the BaseSpecimen itself. Using the BaseSpecimenDefinition act as a component of the Accession act allows the identity of the trial, subject, visit, etc. to flow through automatically to the specimen, and also to the tests and results. When extensions to the model are determined, there will be a choice of SpecimenDefinition acts at this point, allowing variants in the information represented depending on the type of testing to be performed.

The NaturalMaterial (capturing material code, codelist and name from the business model) plays the role of BaseSpecimen, which participates as the subject of the BaseSpecimenDefinition act. The specimen id is in the role itself. Collection start and end dates are captured in the separate SpecimenCollectionProcedure act, which produces the BaseSpecimen. The SpecimenCollection act is in turn related to the ProtocolSpecimenCollection, which defines the planned specimen collection as an offset from a ReferenceEvent. The offset (captured in the pauseQuantity) and the name of the ReferenceEvent (in the code attribute) provide the planned collection time information from the business model.

The date the specimen is received at the laboratory is captured as the activityTime attribute of the ContainerRegistration act. Condition and comments are recorded in the SpecimenComment related to the BaseSpecimen. Subjects's Age and Fasting Status are collected as information pertinent to the specimen collection act itself.

Business Model Category: Base Battery

The name and identifier for each battery performed will be captured in the BaseBattery component of BaseSpecimenDefinition.

Business Model Categories: Base Test and Base Result

Since the business model supports only a single result for each test performed, both of these model categories are represented by the RMIM class BaseUnitaryResult and its related classes.

The performing laboratory is designated as the Laboratory represented by the Agent who actually performs the test. The identity of that Agent is not captured in this message.

The identifiers for the test to be performed are all captured in the code attribute, which has components (by virtue of its datatype) for specifying a test code, a code list for that test code, and any number of translations of that code into other coding systems. Thus the data provider's code can be translated into the data recipient's code, and into the LOINC code all within this same attribute. Each of these codes can also be accompanied by a display name.

Also included in the BaseUnitaryResult class are attributes for additional description of the test (text), the status of the test (statusCode), the start and end dates/times of the testing activity (activityTime), the blinded status of the test result (confidentialityCode), and the test type (reasonCode).

The act ReportToSite, of which the BaseUnitaryResult is the subject, documents the date/time and status of the act of reporting each result to the study site.

Test level comments are captured in the TestComment class as uncoded character strings.

Using the PQ datatype, with it's component PQR translations, the single attribute named "value" captures all representations of the actual test result. The first (PQ) component is reserved for the result represented in the UCUM coding system for unit of measure (UOM). Subsequent translation components using the PQR datatype will represent the result value as SI, conventional and reported, respectively. Each PQR component contains within it components for representing the numeric value of the result, the unit of measure, the unit of measure coding system, and a textual representation of the result. In xml, each result is specified as being of a particular xsitype, which will allow the specification of the Reported Result Type as Coded, Numeric, Text or Range. The methodCode attribute of the BaseUnitaryResult will designate whether the Reported Result should be characterized as "greater than quantifiable limit" or "less that quantifiable limit" -- so the requirements for Reported Result Type are satisfied between these two mechanisms.

Reference range values for each result are captured in the ReferenceRange class, whose value attribute is structured as is the value attribute of the BaseUnitaryResult class. In this case, each type of result (conventional, SI, reported) is expressed as an interval, rather than as a single value, giving the extremes of the range.

The alerts (alert flag, delta flag, exclusion flag), as well as interpretations not specified, are captured in the set of codes included in the interpretationCode of the BaseUnitaryResult. A single toxicity grade is captured as a separate observation.

Business Model Requirement: allow specification of transaction type (update, add, delete, no change) at every level of nesting

This processing specification is handled in the HMD, rather than in the RMIM, and is displayed in the XL View of the HMD. For each of the RMIM acts that represents a nested level of the hierarchy, a default update mode is specified in the HMD, along with the set of allowed update codes for that act. Each Implementation Technology Specification (ITS) is expected to use the functionality available in that technology to validate each update mode specified at run-time, and to communicate that update mode. Each implementation technology will handle the communication of update status differently, depending on the functionality available in the technology.

CTLaboratory Observation RMIM as a Refinement of the Laboratory Domain DMIM

(Note: the description here relates to the Laboratory Domain DMIM as published in HL7 Version3 Ballot Cycle #5 - July, 2003. During that cycle, this specification passed membership-level ballot. The comparison has not been updated since that time.)

This RMIM models both the information superstructure for representing the administrative aspects of a clinical trial (protocols, trials, sites, investigators, subjects and study events), and the information requirements for representing the laboratory observations generated during the conduct of a clinical trial. These later requirements are substantially the same as those supported by the DMIM for the HL7 Laboratory domain, and the observation portion of the RMIM presented here can be substantially derived from that DMIM. The link between the administrative model and the observation model occurs as the observation model class Accession is related to the administrative class StudyEvent. To create the CTLaboratory Observation HMD, the resulting RMIM is traversed beginning in the administrative portion of the model, rather than at the individual observations.

The approach used for observations in the RMIM is to unroll the Observation class of the DMIM to three levels, constraining the DMIM ActRelationship sourceOf / targetOf to the typeCode COMP (component of) for each level.

The top level is the BaseSpecimenDefinition clone in the RMIM. This level implements the componentOf relationship to the Accession class from the DMIM. It also implements the DMIM's subject relationship to the Specimen.

The second level is the BaseBattery clone in the RMIM.

The third level -- the BaseUnitaryResult clone -- represents the individual observations, and implements the Performer and Pertinent Information relationships from the DMIM. This third level implements the sourceOf / targetOf relationship once more, with typeCode constrained to REFV (reference value) to capture the reference ranges for the individual observations.

The RecordTarget (Patient) relationship of the DMIM is represented in the administrative portion of the RMIM by the assignment of each study subject to a trial, trial site and investigator through the StudyAssignment act. This StudyAssignment act inherits the trial and subject contexts, and propagates them to the various levels of observation through the intervening component relationships.

The remaining Observation relationships in the DMIM are not implemented in the RMIM. However, some additional relationships not supported by the DMIM are implemented in the RMIM. These include:

  • the inclusion of information about other "reports" that have been made concerning the observation. When reporting laboratory observations to a clinical trial sponsor, it is customary to include information on when the same observation was reported to the study site.

  • the specification of "update mode". Pending HL7 resolution of how update mode will be handled generally in HL7 messages, the Modeling and Methodology committee has approved the representation employed in the RMIM

The Specimen CMET as a whole was not incorporated into the RMIM, primarily due to that CMET's complexity, but also for reasons of certain incompatibilities between the CMET and the RMIM's business requirements. Instead, portions of the CMET were directly replicated in the RMIM. These portions are:

  • use of the ContainerRegistration clone to capture reciept information for the specimen

  • use of the SpecimenCollectionProcedure clone to capture specimen collection timeframe

  • use of the Natural clone (called NaturalMaterial in the RMIM) for identifying the specimen material. Note, however, the looser constraint on this role player in the RMIM, as compared to the DMIM. When reporting laboratory results to a sponsor it is not generally necessary to restate the material for an identified specimen, since the sponsor receives that information independently from the sutdy site

Different representation approaches were used in:

  • representing specimen observations, specimen treatments and specimen additives, which were bundled together with comments into a class called SpecimenComment

Additional specimen representation requirements include:

  • the need to specify control variables (eg. subject age and fasting status) at the time of the specimen collection. Due to the inheritance of the research subject instance through propagation from the administrative portion of the model, the DMIM approach of specifying this collection-specific information with the subject of the observation is not feasible.

  • the need to relate the specimen collection procedure to the protocol specifications for that procedure
