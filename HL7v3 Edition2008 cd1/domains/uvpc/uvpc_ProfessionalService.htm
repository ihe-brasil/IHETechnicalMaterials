Introduction

Professional services deals with the capturing of information about specific activities done "to" a patient such as training, assessments, counseling, physiotherapy and surgery. It excludes activities meant to evaluate a characteristic of a patient (i.e. all observations), though interventions such as colonoscopies which include removal of samples may be classified as professional services. It also excludes the administration of drugs or vaccines, as these are handled by more specialized transactions.

The purpose of the transactions is to capture the discrete encoded data associated with the service such as what was done, where, when, by whom, etc. More specifically this topic covers all transactions related to professional services, including:

  • Recording professional services performed, for example education, training, smoking cessation, physiotherapy and surgery for the patient in the EHR.
  • Retrieving a patient's professional service records - retrieve a list of the professional services performed for a specific patient, potentially filtered by time or other criteria.

The interactions are crafted to support a 'request-based' architecture in which messages are sent from a point of service (POS) such as a clinic to a Shared Health Record (SHR). These messages are phrased as requests because the SHR reserves the right to refuse all requests. Reasons might include issues such as lack of permission, lack of recorded patient consent, etc.

Further guidance to the intended usage of these transactions:

  • Narrative surgery reports should be handled via the Clinical Observations transactions, see Clinical Document Topic. (Note: Note yet normative and not included in this release.)

  • If the patient has had a simple endoscopy or colonoscopy the Record Patient Coded Observation should be used. Common Observations has been incorporated into the Observations Domain. (Note: Note yet normative and not included in this release.)

Focal Class

The Procedure Event act is used to describe all aspects of professional services like the name of healthcare professionals, specific service received and patient details. This central act has relationships to several other acts. Like all patient care RMIMs, most of these relationships connect directly to information pertinent to care provisioning, such as supporting clinical information, comments, referrals, status change information, etc. It also has Participations which define roles of entities, e.g. the role of patient or provider of the service.

Associations

The Professional Service focal class is surrounded by the following associations:

  • Participation to "ProviderOrPatientOrRelated" choice box (author, performer, responsibleParty, informant, or patient)
  • Participation to "EHR Repository" (custodian1)
  • Participation to "Record Location" (custodian2)
  • Participation to "Service Delivery Location" (location)
  • The "Has Predecessor" ActRelationship to an Old Procedure Event describing Id of the record it succeeded (predecessor)
  • The "Has Successor" ActRelationship to a New Procedure Event containing the Id of the succeeding record (successor)
  • The "Has Subject Of" ActRelationship to Annotation Indicator signifying whether or not there are annotations attached to the professional service record (subjectOf2)
  • The "Is Component Of" ActRelationship to WorkingListEvent describing category of the service (componentOf1)
  • The " Subject Of" ActRelationship to DocumentEvent containing the Id of the referenced Service Report (subjectOf3)
  • The "Is Subject Of" ActRelationship to A_Annotation CMET (subjectOf1) containing comments that have been made about the professional service record
  • The "Has Reason" ActRelationship to A_SupportingClinicalInformation CMET (reason) containing clinical information to support the need for the service
  • The "Is Component Of" ActRelationship to A_Encounter CMET indicating the clinicall event group to which this professional service is linked(componentOf2)
  • The "Is Fulfillment Of" ActRelationship to ActRequest indicating the prior request for which this professional is fulfilling (inFulfillmentOf)

Participations

author (provider): This identifies the provider who is responsible for the decision to capture the Professional Service in the patient's EHR. Note that while the author is frequently the data-enterer, this will not always be the case, for example where transcribers are involved.

performer (Provider): Identifies the person(s) who actually performed the action described in the record.

responsibleParty (Provider): This identifies the person that has primary responsibility for the actions of the author in creating the Professional Service. The supervising provider is not necessarily present when the action is performed, but is professionally accountable for the action through the power to delegate, and the duty to review actions with the performing actor after the fact.

subject (Patient): This identifies the patient associated with the Professional Service record.

informant (Provider, Patient, or RelatedParty): In the case where the information in the record is being captured second-hand, this identifies the source of the information. If not specified, it is assumed that the author is capturing information of which they have first-hand awareness.

custodian1: This indicates the EHR repository the Professional Service record is stored in.

custodian2: This indicates the physical facility where the Professional Service record is stored. This is generally the same as the location where the action was performed, but in some cases may be different. (E.g. where the action was done at the patient's home, but the data is captured and stored in the clinician's clinic. Used to indicate where records are likely kept for follow-up.

location: This identifies the location where the Professional Service actually occurred. E.g. Patient's residence, clinic, work site, etc.

ActRelationships

predecessor: Identifies any records that are "superseded" by the current record. This will cause the referenced records to be marked as "obsolete" with a reference pointing to this record. Used in circumstances where information initially captured about an event is erroneous, incomplete or not captured at the desired level of detail and the change cannot be made by retracting the original record

successor: Identifies the record that supersedes the current record. This attribute is set when a new record identifies the current record as being replaced. Used in circumstances where a newer or corrected version of the record of this event exists.

subjectOf2: This association provides an indication that there are notes/comment records associated with the Professional Service record. Used to show whether additional information is available on further drill-down.

subjectOf4: Describes information associated with status change for the professional service record.

componentOf1: Describes the categorization of the service. E.g. Psychological Counseling, Smoking Cessation, Cardiac Surgeries, etc.

subjectOf3: Indicates an identifier for a report associated with the professional service.

subjectOf1: This indicates comments that providers have recorded about the Professional Service.

reason: Identifies the clinical reason(s) for performing the action described by the record. Essential for understanding the context in which the action was performed.

component2: This identifies the encounter-based, condition-based and/or other care-based collections that the Professional Service is considered to be part of. Care compositions (defined by A_CaredEntity CMET) provide a means of navigating a patient's EHR by grouping related records together into identified "collections"".

inFulfillmentOf: This ties the record to the request (referral or specific request) that resulted in the creation of the Professional Service record. By requesting the action, a provider takes a level of responsibility for it. Also, a link between request and fulfilling record may result in differences in query responses.

Professional Service R-MIMs

The specific messages defined for the recording or listing of professional services may be slightly constrained versions of the generic professional service structures described above. Following is the list of professional service RMIMs, identifying the those components of the professional service generic structure that have been constrained out:

REPC_RM610001 - Professional Service Record

This is the message payload for recording professional service for the first time. Thus, the following is a list of deviations from the full complement of professional service structure as described above:

  • Service Record Id (ProcedureEvent.Id) is not specified
  • Author and ResponsibleParty participations are constrained out (sent in message as part of ControlActWrapper)
  • The 'Replaced by Record' class is not specified
  • Cardinality of the subjectOf1 actRelationship is set to [0..1]
  • Service Category (WorkingListEvent) is not specified

REPC_RM610002 - Professional Service List

This is the message payload for a response to a query for a patient's professional service summary information. This message comprises the full complement of classes, association, participations and attributes as described above for the generic Professional Service message structure.

REPC_RM610004 - Professional Service List Query

This is not a constrained version of the generic Professional Service message structure. Rather, it is a message that identifies the various parameters that act as filters on the records to be retrieved. Allows the user and/or the point-of-service application to constrain what Professional Service information they wish to retrieve.
