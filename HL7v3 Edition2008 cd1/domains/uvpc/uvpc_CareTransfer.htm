Introduction

This topic covers the business cycle of requests and promises that are included in negotiations regarding the transfer of responsibility for care.

Traditionally, this set of communications has been called a "referral." However, referrals tend to have specific meanings within various cultures, and an attempt has been made to create an overall definition of referral that is quite general. In order to avoid confusion among cultures, the individual messages do not use the term "referral". Rather they identify specific use cases and triggers related to communications that use the HL7 RIM "Act" class of "CareProvision (PCPR)" as the "entry point" of the refined message information model (RMIM) for Care Transfer message development.

Care Transfer Definition

Care Transfer is the communication between an Author and a Health Care Provider with the intent of negotiating an assumption of responsibility for the care by the receiver of the request. An assumption of responsibility is constrained to a statement of the scope of the responsibility including the type of care, the specific conditions named, and the specific care plans named, where:

'Communication' may be by way of

  • messaging with or without an attached clinical document, e.g care transfer summary.

'Communication' may be triggered by

  • request for shared care (partial transfer of care responsibility)
  • request for complete transfer of care responsibility

'Author' of care transfer communications can be

  • clinicians
  • healthcare agencies (including district nursing, community health, social welfare)
  • patient (self referral)
  • patient family/significant others
  • court or other non health care organization
  • devices authorized by organizations

'Health care providers' can be

  • clinicians
  • healthcare agencies (including district nursing, community health, social welfare)

Purpose

This storyboard demonstrates the communication flow between a client/resident or their advocate requesting a change to an existing service.

 7.1.1.1Request for Changes in Ongoing Services (REPC_SN004008UV01)
Precondition: Mr. Adam Everyman, who lives at home receives the equivalent of one meal a day from the meals on wheels service operated by the Green Acres Retirement Home (GARH) group. His daughter, who pays the client contribution towards the cost of this service, notes he is continuing to lose weight and persuades Mr. Everyman to change his current order to two meals a day.

Activities: On behalf of her father, Nancy Nuclear sends a request to GARH's hospitality service with a request for changes to her father's existing meals service [Interaction: Revise Care Transfer Request ]. Before the catering services client manager can arrange the additional meal for Mr. Everyman, Ms. Nuclear is required to authorize the additional client contribution. The Dietician, Connie Chow sends an authorization request to Ms. Nuclear. Ms. Nuclear is happy to pay the extra contribution for the additional daily meal and advises Connie Chow to proceed. Upon receipt of the authorization acceptance, the hospitality service confirms the new arrangements will be put into place e.g. arrange the additional meal [Interaction Replace Care Transfer Promiseare in place for Mr. Everyman's meals by sending a new care plan to Ms. Nuclear [Interaction: Replace Care Provision ].

Postcondition: Arrangements are in place for Mr. Everyman to receive two meals per day, with the revised invoice going to his daughter. Having made the necessary arrangements with the meals on wheels delivery service, Connie Chow's ordering systems automatically sends a message to the GARH client account system advising of the additional charges to be invoiced to Ms. Nuclear as the designated payor for Mr. Everyman.

Update Requirements Summary: The communicators of this storyboard, Ms. Nuclear, on behalf of her father, and the dietician, Connie Chow, are focused on ensuring that requested changes to ongoing services (in this case catering) are actioned in a timely manner, with documentation of appropriate authorizations.

 7.1.2.1Care Transfer Completed Speciality Care (REPC_SN004001UV01)
Precondition: Dr. Patricia Primary is a primary care physician who has been seeing a patient regularly for some time and utilizing her standard care plans for the condition. The patient's condition is worsening, and Dr. Primary wishes to obtain the opinion of a gastroenterologist specialist, Dr. Tony Tum.

Activities: Dr. Primary prepares a summary from the existing electronic medical records highlighting the reason for the consultation, including summaries of the other medical conditions for which the patient receives care, and including the medication list, allergy list, and lists of prior surgeries and hospitalizations. This summary is sent to Dr. Tum along with a request to see the patient [Interaction: Care Transfer Request]. Dr. Tum decides to see the patient and sends the appropriate response back to Dr. Primary [Interaction: Care Transfer Promise]. Dr. Tum then sees the patient, prepares a summary of his evaluation and opinions on change of treatment [Interaction: Complete Care Provision]. Dr. Tum sends the summary to Dr. Primary, who alters the care plans with the recommended changes.

Alternative flows:

  1. Dr. Tum may decline the request to see the patient [Interaction: Reject Care Transfer] >>Post-Condition: Dr. Primary tries another gastroenterologist specialist.
  2. Dr. Tum may send recommendations without seeing the patient (paper consultation).
  3. Dr. Tum may request more information before seeing the patient (not illustrated).
  4. Dr. Primary may send more information without a request for more information [Interaction: Revise Care Transfer Request].

    Postcondition: Dr. Primary returns to seeing the patient on a regular basis, now utilizing the altered care plans for the patient.

    Updates Requirements Summary: The communicators of these messages, Dr. Primary and Dr. Tum are focused on the referral request from Dr. Primary and the outcome of that referral back to Dr. Primary. This includes the capacity for Dr. Primary to provide updated information to Dr. Tum as it comes to hand during the consultation.



7.1.3Multidisciplinary Care Provision  (REPC_ST004003UV01)

Purpose

This storyboard demonstrates the communication flow associated with requests by a Primary Care Physician (GP) to other clinical or allied health care providers to contribute to the delivery of a multidisciplinary care plan, such as a Chronic Disease Management care plan (CDM). CDMs are designed to improve the medical management of care for a person with chronic or complex care needs. (In Australia the GP is encouraged to collaborate with other providers if the patient has complex care needs which would benefit form multidisciplinary care team input. Similar initiatives exist in many countries.)

 7.1.3.1Multidisciplinary Care Provision (REPC_SN004003UV01)

Precondition: Dr Patricia Primary is preparing a multidisciplinary care plan for Mr Adam Everyman to improve his health outcomes and quality of life goals associated with his diabetes and hypertension. She recommends to Mr Everyman involving a physiotherapist, a nutritionist and his daughter, Nancy Nuclear. Dr Primary obtains Mr Everyman's authorization to share his medical history and goals with the other providers and his daughter.

Activities:

Request shared care provision

Dr Primary sends an individual request to Physiotherapist Seth Stretcher, Nutritionist Connie Chow and to Mr Everyman's daughter, Nancy Nuclear [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ].

Each in turn send a response to confirm they will make arrangements to meet with Mr Everyman to develop a plan to meet the particular goals specified by Dr Primary [Interaction: Care Transfer Promise].

In turn Seth Stretcher and Connie Chow meet with Mr Everyman and his daughter to review his goals in relation to his diabetes and hypertension. Each prepares a care plan for Mr Everyman which they summarise and forward to Dr Primary [Interaction: Activate Care Provision]. Dr Primary distills these contributions into an overall Chronic Disease Management care plan and provides copies to each of the members of the multidisciplinary care planning team including Mr Everyman's daughter, who under the plan has been identified as responsible for assisting Mr Everyman with his meals [Interaction: Revise Care Transfer Request ] each acknowledge their undertaking to action the revised, composite care plan [Interaction: Replace Care Transfer Promise].

Ongoing shared care provision

Each provider continues to see or communicate with Mr Everyman to review his response to the respective components to the plan of care. They provide a summary report on progress to Dr Primary [Interaction: Append Care Provision ].

Review shared care provision (not shown on Interaction Diagram)

Three weeks later, Dr Primary meets with Mr Everyman and his daughter to review progress in the management of his diabetes and hypertension. While he has achieved some of his goals, Mr Everyman is disappointed in the progress with those relating to stabilizing his blood sugar levels. Dr Primary amends the goals for Mr Everyman and sends these to Connie Chow and Nancy Nuclear [Interaction: Revise Care Transfer Request ].

Connie Chow and Nancy Nuclear review their care plans for Mr Everyman in light of the new goals from Dr Primary and provide ongoing progress reports on the new care plans [Interaction: Replace Care Provision].

Postcondition: Mr Everyman receives team based care, coordinated and reviewed by his Primary Care Physician, Dr Primary.

Updates Requirement Summary: The participants of this storyboard are Dr Primary, Physiotherapist Seth Stretcher, Nutritionist Connie Chow and the patient's daughter, Nancy Nuclear. They are focused on responding to a request by Dr Primary to collaborate on the development of an integrated care plan for Mr Everyman to improve his health outcomes and quality of life goals associated with his diabetes and hypertension.

 7.1.4.1Referral for Aging Services (REPC_SN004005UV01)

Precondition:Peter Process is the social worker at Good Health Hospital (GHH) responsible for coordinating in patient discharges for older patients. His role is to maximize the successful return of frail elderly inpatients to the community, including to residential aged care services. He works closely with the ageing services (home care and residential) in the surrounding area. He is looking for a nursing home placement for a Mr. Everyman who requires some intensive rehabilitation following a fall before he can safely return to living with his daughter who works full time. He recommends Green Acres Retirement Home (GARH) as a suitable provider organization to Mr. Everyman and his daughter, Nancy Nuclear, who authorizes him to approach GARH on his behalf, along with three other nursing homes within 5 miles radius of Ms Nuclear's home.

Activities:

Send referral

Peter Process forwards a request for a temporary nursing home placement for Mr. Everyman to Alice Admitter commencing the process of referral for ageing services. [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ]. As she is processing the referral Alice Admitter notes Peter Process has not included all of Mr. Everyman's insurance details. She sends the request back to Peter Process, indicating the request cannot be processed in its current form [Interaction: Reject Care Transfer ].

Confirm undertaking to process request

Alice Admitter accepts the referral for processing by identifying the request as 'Accepted' (or active) in the GARH admissions database. This triggers an automatic progress report on the referral to Peter Process at GHH [Interaction: Notify Care Transfer Request ]. She then forwards the request to Nancy Nightingale to review [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ].

Query referral information

Nancy Nightingale sends a request for further clinical information on Mr. Everyman's cognitive functional capacity to Peter Process at GHH who on this occasion had not attached the full cognitive assessment findings. [Interaction: Care Provision Event, Query.] As he had already obtained Mr. Everyman's consent to share his assessment findings with other service providers, Peter Process attaches the summary of Mr. Everyman's cognitive and behavioral assessment, including the scores from the assessments undertaken in the hospital of his cognitive, behavioral and mood status. He sends this to Nancy Nightingale. [Interaction: Care Provision Event Query, Response]. (Note these query interactions are out of scope of current storyboard).

Advise vacancy available

Following her review of Mr. Everyman's rehabilitation and care needs, Nancy Nightingale is happy to offer the one remaining vacant bed at GARH to Mr. Everyman. She advises Alice Admitter of this decision [Interaction: Care Transfer Promise ]. Alice Admitter promptly notifies Peter Process of the vacancy [Interaction: Care Transfer Promise ].

This action simultaneously updates GARH's 'Active Referrals' file, maintained by Alice Admitter, completing the audit trail for this request for services. Mr. Everyman's name is not removed from the 'Active Referral's file until notification is received that all contractual documentation is in place and that he has actually been admitted to GARH.

Alternative flows:

  1. Peter Process receives a call from Mr. Everyman's daughter to advise she is taking parental leave for two months so her father can be discharged directly home to live with her. Following the leave (when she returns to work) she will reassess whether her father still needs to receive formal ageing services. Peter Process immediately notifies Alice Admitter and Nancy Nightingale that the request for services for Mr. Everyman is withdrawn [Interaction: Abort Care Transfer Request ]. >>Postcondition: Peter Process discharges Mr. Everyman home.
  2. Having read the detailed cognitive and behavioral assessment report for Mr. Everyman, Nancy Nightingale determines the respite bed she currently has available (for short term admission only) will not be adequate to meet the rehabilitation goals set for Mr. Everyman. She notifies Alice Admitter who in turn notifies Peter Process, Mr. Everyman and his daughter of the outcome of the decision. [Interaction: Reject Care Transfer ]. >>Postcondition: Peter Process contacts the other nursing home facilities in the area in the hope of finding a suitable placement for Mr. Everyman.
  3. Alice Admitter advises Ms Nuclear that GARH will be able to offer the next permanent nursing home bed to her father when it becomes available but she is unable to say precisely when that will be. Ms Nuclear is delighted and happy to wait on a place as GARH is her father's preferred choice of nursing home. Alice Admitter adds Mr. Everyman's name to the GARH Waiting List for permanent placement. She notifies Peter Process of this outcome as Mr. Everyman has yet to be discharged from hospital [Interaction: Care Transfer Promise ]. >>Postcondition: Peter Process delays Mr. Everyman's discharge in the hope a bed becomes available for him at GARH.

Update Requirements Summary: The communicators of these messages, Peter Process (on behalf of Mr Brown), Alice Admitter and Nancy Nightingale are focused on the referral request designed to locate an aged care placement as smoothly and quickly as possible, whilst ensuring the suitability of that placement.

 7.1.5.1Aged Care (Lite) Transfer (REPC_SN004006UV01)

Precondition: Peter Process, Discharge Planner at Good Health Hospital is looking for a nursing home placement for Mr. Adam Everyman, 88 yrs in-patient close to where his son lives. He sends requests to transfer Mr. Everyman to five nursing homes, including Green Acres Retirement Home (GARH), where the Admissions Officer is Alice Admitter.

Activities:

Service Provision Transfer (Referral for service)

Acting on Mr. Everyman's authorization, Peter Process forwards a request for services, including a copy of Mr. Everyman's eligibility assessment, to five nursing homes in closest proximity to where his son lives. [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ]. He receives automatic acknowledgement from four of these nursing homes that his request has been received. Three weeks later, Peter Process receives advice from Alice Admitter, that Mr. Everyman has been offered a nursing home place at GARH and that he is to be admitted the next day. [Interaction: Care Transfer Promise ].

Service Provision Transfer Withdrawal

Having secured a place for Mr. Everyman at GARH, Peter Process, immediately notifies the three other nursing homes of his withdrawal of his Request for Service for Mr. Everyman. [Interaction: Abort Care Transfer Request ].

Postcondition: Peter Process makes arrangements for Mr. Everyman's discharge and transfer to GARH, having withdrawn his requests from the other nursing homes.

Updates Requirement Summary: Having successfully found a place for Mr. Everyman, Peter Process is able to focus on placement of other elderly inpatients. His information system gives him some information (via an audit trail) of how long respective nursing homes are taking to respond to requests for service. He uses this information to target his next requests.

 7.1.6.1Care Transfer Request Medication Chart (REPC_SN004004UV01)
Precondition: Nancy Nightingale, RN at Green Acres Retirement Home, is concerned that Mr. Everyman's pain management is no longer effective. She feels a review of all his medications might be in order. She discusses her concerns with the Palliative Care Clinical Consultant who agrees a review of all Mr. Everyman's current medication is a good idea.

Activities: Nancy Nightingale sends a request to Dr. Patricia Primary outlining the need for a review of Mr. Everyman's medication chart. She provides a copy of the 24 hour pain chart findings which demonstrates Mr. Everyman is experiencing too frequent breakthrough of pain [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ].

Dr. Primary notifies Nancy Nightingale she will visit Mr. Everyman later that day [Interaction: Care Transfer Promise ]. Dr. Primary visits Mr. Everyman later that day and with Nancy Nightingale reviews all Mr. Everyman's medications. She provides a revised Medication Chart for Mr. Everyman [Interaction: Medication Administration Order Activate, Fulfillment Request]. As Nancy Nightingale uploads the new medication chart into the electronic drug chart system an automatic message is sent to the off-site Pharmacist's System to notify the changed orders for Mr. Everyman [Interaction: Combined Order Activate, Fulfillment Request]. (N.B Medication order messages are out of scope of this storyboard.)

Alternative view: Dr. Primary receives the request from Nancy Nightingale but feels a review is not required at this time [Interaction: Reject Care Transfer ].

Postcondition: Mr. Everyman commences on a new medication regime that same evening. Nancy Nightingale continues the pain management chart and over the next 12 hours Mr. Everyman reports no pain breakthrough with no loss of concentration.

Update Requirements Summary: The communicators of this message, RN, Nancy Nightingale and Dr. Primary, are focused on requesting and completing a review of Mr. Everyman's medication chart. The outcome of the review is communicated to the off-site pharmacy so that the new supplies can be provided directly to the nursing home in a timely manner.

 7.1.7.1Pastoral Care (REPC_SN004007UV01)

Precondition: Mrs. Eve Everywoman is a resident in the Green Acres Retirement Home (GARH) who is receiving palliative care. She has insight into her deteriorating condition which is causing her high stress and anxiety. Her husband, Neville Nuclear, feels she has unresolved issues she needs to deal with in relation to her eldest daughter, with whom she has been estranged for the past five years. Mr. Nuclear feels this anxiety is counteracting the pain medication she is taking. He is aware that GARH has an excellent pastoral care service, which draws upon trained volunteers from local organizations, including the local Catholic Church. He discusses with his wife the idea of contacting someone for her to talk with "in private" about their daughter.

Activities:

Request spiritual needs support

Larry Listener, Director of Pastoral Care, receives a call from Mrs. Everywoman's husband requesting pastoral care services for his wife. As GARH is part of the region managed by Pastoral Care Coordinator Helen Helper, Larry Listener sends a note to ask Helen to arrange services for Mrs. Everywoman [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ]. Helen Helper confirms with Larry Listener that she has received the request and that she has made an appointment to visit Mrs. Everywoman the following day [Interaction: Care Transfer Promise ].

Assign pastoral care support worker

Following her visit and assessment of Mrs. Everywoman, Helen Helper prepares a care plan for Mrs. Everywoman based on their discussions. She checks her list of pastoral care volunteers to see who has the capacity to take on Mrs. Everywoman's care. As she only meets with her volunteers monthly Helen Helper sends a request to Valerie Volunteer, who is both a Catholic and whose case-load has recently been reduced. She asks Valerie if she is available to take on a new client, providing details of Mrs. Everywoman's location and the contact details of the Residential Care Coordinator [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ].

Valerie Volunteer accesses the request via her mobile phone. She notifies Helen Helper that she is able to take on Mrs. Everywoman, subject to Mrs. Everywoman's acceptance [Interaction: Care Transfer Promise ].

Helen Helper advises Larry Listener that Valerie Volunteer has been assigned to Mrs. Everywoman and that a care plan is in place that has been agreed to with Mrs. Everywoman. A copy of this message is sent to Nancy Nightingale, the Residential Care Coordinator, so that she can upload Valerie Volunteer's contact details and a copy of the pastoral care plan into the nursing documentation system [Interaction: Care Transfer Promise ].

Pastoral care provision ongoing

Valerie Volunteer commences visits with Mrs. Everywoman. With reference to the pastoral care plan, she documents each visit incorporating information on the duration of her visit (detailed notes of their discussions are not recorded). The care documentation system automatically sends a summary of Valerie Volunteer's initial visit to Helen Helper. [Interaction: Activate Care Provision].This allows Helen Helper to monitor the quality and level of activity of her team of pastoral care volunteers, in addition to providing an audit trail of this and subsequent pastoral care activities [Interaction: Append Care Provision].

Suspension of pastoral care services

After six visits Mrs. Everywoman is reconciled with her daughter. Mrs. Everywoman requests a temporary suspension of visits while she enjoys time with her daughter. Valerie Volunteer documents this outcome in Mrs. Everywoman's records and sends a summary of the last visit to Helen Helper who records a requested suspension in pastoral care services. [Interaction: Suspend Care Provision]. In turn she sends a copy of this message to the Director of Pastoral Care, Larry Listener [Interaction: Suspend Care Provision].

Postcondition: Documentation of the impact of the pastoral care services provided to Mrs. Everywoman by Valerie Volunteer appears in the nursing documentation. In addition, Helen Helper has been able to maintain an arms length observation of the frequency and duration of Valerie Volunteer's visits. Should Mrs. Everywoman request further pastoral care services, the relevant details of this recent service will be available for the requesting staff member.

Updates Requirement Summary: The communicators of this interaction, the Director of Pastoral Care, a Regional Pastoral Care Coordinator and the carer providing the actual Pastoral Care, are focused on responding to requests for the provision of pastoral care and on monitoring who and when pastoral care services are being provided.

 7.1.8.1Surgical Referral (REPC_SN004002UV01)
Precondition: Mr. Adam Everyman is an inpatient at Good Health Hospital (GHH). His physician, Dr. Aaron Attend, refers him for thoracic surgery to Dr. Cutter who agrees to perform surgery on Mr. Everyman, adding him to his surgical list at GHH on June 27. Following the thoracic surgery to rule out lung cancer, Dr. Cutter considers that the surgical results indicate Mr. Everyman has lung cancer. He refers Mr. Everyman for a second opinion to Dr. Dunst, a pulmonologist, before proceeding with ongoing care by himself in the Gamma Cancer Clinic. Dr. Cutter also refers Mr. Everyman to the Quit Now for Smoking cessation education program.

Activities: Dr. Attend refers Mr. Everyman to Dr. Cutter with a request for thoracic surgery to rule out lung cancer. He includes a health summary as part of this request [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ]. Dr Cutter acknowledges this request and confirms he has added Mr Everyman to his surgical list for 27 June [Interaction Care Transfer Promise].

Following the thoracic on Mr. Everyman Dr. Cutter refers Mr. Everyman for a second opinion to Dr. Dunst. Dr. Cutter compiles a summary of Mr. Everyman's surgery along with the health summary provided by Dr. Attend and sends one referral request to Dr. Dunst and another to the Quit Now for Smoking cessation education program [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ], both acknowledge the request to Dr Cutter [Interaction: Care Transfer Promise].

Postcondition: Having had concerns about his patient, Dr Attend has successfully referred Mr. Everyman to Dr. Cutter.

Update Requirements Summary: The actors for this communication are Drs. Attend, Cutter, Dunst and the Quit Now clinic. The foci of the communication are Dr. Attend's health summary and referral request to Dr. Cutter, Dr. Cutter's referral request and inpatient surgical summary sent to Dr. Dunst and the Quit Now clinic.

7.2.1Care Transfer Placer  (REPC_AR002030UV01)
Description 
Structured Name:  Care Transfer Request Placer

The application role includes the behaviors needed to create, communicate and appropriately manage a care transfer request. This includes the ability to transmit a care transfer request as a notification or a fulfillment request, and to receive the request fulfiller application's acceptance or rejection of the request.

7.2.2Care Transfer Fulfiller  (REPC_AR002040UV01)
Description 
Structured Name:  Care Transfer Request Fulfiller

The application role includes the behaviors needed to receive, communicate subsequent transactions, and appropriately manage care transfer request. This includes the ability to accept a care transfer request and to provide a rejection response if appropriate.

7.2.3Care Transfer Tracker  (REPC_AR002020UV01)
Description 
Structured Name:  Care Transfer Request Tracker

An application that is capable of receiving a notification from another system about a care transfer request.

Description

Care Transfer Request RMIM Overview

This model captures the data and associations that are needed when a care transfer is requested. This model, and its accompanying diagram, is very similar to the Care Provision DMIM but uses "local CMETs" to abstract the complexity of the care structures associated with the Care Provision Request Act. The reader should refer to the DMIM discussion before reviewing the RMIM followed by the discussion for each care structure. The discussion of this model takes up features that do not explicitly exist in the DMIM, and were not directly addressed in the review of that model.

Care Provision Request Focal Class

As noted in the discussion of the DMIM, a Care Provision Request is a request to transfer the responsibility of Care Provision or more simply a Care Transfer Request. Typically the request is made by a clinician within an application and transmitted to the performing party via a messaging interface.

All the participations encountered in this model have already been addressed in the discussion of the DMIM.

Associations

The following associations are defined for a Care Provision Request. Each association captures the relationship between the Care Provision Request and an entity or act that plays a significant part in the Care Transfer.

Participations

recordTarget: the record target is the entity for which the Care Transfer Request is being recorded. There can only be one record target for a Care Transfer Request. The target entity information is captured within the R_Patient CMET or R_CaredEntity local CMET. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

subject: the subject or subjects that is the target for the care transfer being requested when the subject is not the record target. The subject information is captured within the R_Patient CMET or R_CaredEntity local CMET. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

author: the party that makes the Care Transfer Request. This may be an assigned person or organization (R_AssignedParty) such as a healthcare provider, a related party (R_RelatedParty) such as a family member or court, or the patient (R_Patient) themselves. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

dataEnterer: the transcriptionist who enters the information of the Care Transfer Request. The data enterer information is captured within the R_AssignedPerson CMET.

verifier: the supervisor who verifies the request. There is support for more than one level of verification. The verifying person information is captured within the R_AssignedPerson CMET.

performer: the party who takes part in actually carrying out the requested care provision. There may be multiple performers because several parties may collaborate in the requested care provision. This may be an assigned person or organization (R_AssignedParty) such as a healthcare provider, a related party (R_RelatedParty) such as a family member, or the patient (R_Patient) themselves. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

primaryInformationRecipient: the party who is intended to receive the requested care transfer. This recipient is not the requested performer of the care provision but is receiving the request for their information. This may be an assigned person or organization (R_AssignedParty) such as a healthcare provider, a related party (R_RelatedParty) such as a family member or court, or the patient (R_Patient) themselves. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

Act Relationships

component1: links the Care Provision Request to the care plan that is requested to be performed during the intended patient care provision. Refer to A_CarePlan in the Care Structures topic for further description of this local CMET.

pertinentInformation1: provides pertinent care plans to the care transfer, indicating that these are the other acts that are related but not directly requested as part of the care transfer. Refer to A_CarePlan in the Care Structures topic for further description of this local CMET.

pertinentInformation3: a set of pertinent clinical statements provided as information to support the care transfer. Refer to A_CareStatement in the Care Structures topic for further description of this local CMET.

reason: relationship to a condition recorded as the primary reason for the care transfer. A problem, not specifically a diagnosis, which is the primary reason for the care transfer can also be presented using this relationship. Refer to A_ConditionTrackingEvent in the Care Structures topic for further description of this local CMET.

component2: a collection of clinical statements can be associated as part of the Care Provision Request. This is intended to indicate that, for example, this list of conditions are being managed as part of the requested Care Transfer. Refer to A_StatementCollector in the Care Structures topic for further description of this local CMET.

pertinentInformation2: a Care Transfer Request may have relevant information that is in the form of existing Care Records, which can be attached to the Care Provision Request. Refer to the Care Record topic for further description of this local CMET.

reference: the encounter in which the care transfer was requested, represented by the A_Encounter CMET.

replacementOf: a link to a replaced Care Provision Request. The Care Provision Request being the target of this act relationship has a status of obsolete and can either contain just the instance identifier of the replaced request or the entire request details. NOTE: This relationship should not be used as the Obsolete/Replace Interaction is not currently supported.

Description

Care Transfer Promise RMIM Overview

This model captures the data and associations that are needed when a Care Transfer is promised or accepted.

CareProvisionPromise Focal Class

A Care Provision Promise is an announcement of the acceptance of taking on the responsibility of Care Provision as stated in the fulfilled request. Typically the assigned person responsible for performing the Care Provision fulfilled by the request is the author of the Care Transfer Promise. It is assumed the request is recorded within an application and transmitted to the performing party via a messaging interface; in which case the promise is sent as a acceptance to that request. Only the minimum information required to promise to fulfill the request is provided in the current model, as it was seen to be not necessary to replicate the request detail in the case where requests were not received electronically.

The Care Transfer Promise RMIM can be used to accept/promise or reject/refuse a previously directed request for care provision.

Associations

The following associations are defined for a Care Transfer Promise. Each association captures the relationship between the Care Transfer Promise and an entity or act that plays a significant part in the Care Transfer Promise.

Participations

recordTarget: the record target is the entity for which the Care Transfer Promise is being made. There can only be one record target for a Care Transfer Promise. The target entity information is captured within the R_Patient CMET or R_CaredEntity local CMET. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

subject: the subject is the target for which Care Provision Promise is made when the subject is not the record target. The subject information is captured within the R_Patient CMET or R_CaredEntity local CMET. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

author: the provider that makes the Care Transfer Promise to take on the responsibility of Care Provision, who is analogous to a "fulfiller". This may be an assigned person or organization (R_AssignedParty) such as a healthcare provider, a related party (R_RelatedParty) such as a family member, or the patient (R_Patient) themselves. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

dataEnterer: the transcriptionist who enters the information of the Care Transfer Promise. The data enterer information is captured within the R_AssignedPerson CMET.

verifier: the supervisor who verifies the promise. There is support for more than one level of verification. The verifying party information is captured within the R_AssignedPerson CMET.

performer: the provider who is promising to carry out the Care Provision. There may be multiple performers because several parties may collaborate in the responsibility of Care Provision. This may be an assigned person or organization (R_AssignedParty) such as a healthcare provider, a related party (R_RelatedParty) such as a family member, or the patient (R_Patient) themselves. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

primaryInformationRecipient: the provider who is intended to receive the acceptance of the Care Transfer. The recipient is usually the author of the associated care transfer request, which may be represented by an assigned person or organization (R_AssignedParty), a related party (R_RelatedParty) such as a family member or court, or the patient (R_Patient) themselves. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

Act Relationships

InFulfillmentOf: a Care Transfer Promise is associated with the Care Transfer Request being accepted, that is the Care Transfer Request that the promise fulfills. Only the id attribute is provided to link the request to the promise.

ReplacementOf: a link to a replaced Care Transfer Promise. The Care Transfer Promise being the target of this act relationship has a status of obsolete and can either contain just the unique identifier of the replaced promise or its entire details.
