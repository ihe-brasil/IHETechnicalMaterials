Introduction

This topic covers the summary and record extract notifications that are used to communicate the care actually given while a subject was under the care of a responsible party.

This is an interaction diagram illustrating all the Interactions and Application Roles relevant to the Care Record Topic.

5.1.1Mandatory Care Activation  (REPC_ST002006UV01)

Purpose

The purpose of this storyboard is to illustrate the report messaging related to a mandatory activation of care, for example primary care physcian (General Practitioner (GP)) report of care to a community agency, eg clinic.

Interaction List
Report Care Provision REPC_IN004014UV01

 5.1.1.1Notify Care Provision Summary (REPC_SN002006UV01)

Activities: Dr. Primary is writing Care Provision summaries for use by the Home Health Care Clinic, describing the care of the patient to date. Dr. Primary records the date of onset of Care Provision by her practice. All information including problem lists, allergy lists, medication lists, progress notes, plans for care, and all other information included in her record is included in the summary.

Postcondition: The Care Provision summary for Mr. Adam Everyman is received by the Home Health Care Clinic who implements care according to the plans of care detailed by Dr. Primary.

Update Requirements Summary: The main communicators in this message are Dr. Primary and the receiving clinician (probably a nurse) in the Home Health Care Clinic (HHCC). The focus of the communication is the provision of data to enable the HHCC to provide ongoing care to Mr Adam Everyman.

Whilst not illustrated here, Dr Primary's clinical system maintains a record of when the Care Provision summary was sent to the HHCC.

5.1.2Temporary Handover of Care  (REPC_ST002005UV01)

Purpose

The purpose of this storyboard is to illustrate the report messaging related to a temporary handing over of responsibility for care by a care provider who is taking a vacation to another care provider.

Interaction List
Suspend Care Provision REPC_IN004310UV01

 5.1.2.1Temporary Handover of Care (REPC_SN002005UV01)

Precondition: Mr. Adam Everyman, a 75-year old male patient, is receiving peritoneal dialysis on a trial basis in the assisted living facility where he lives, Home Away from Home, on the orders of Dr. Roy Renal, his nephrologist. After three months, Mr. Everyman appears to be well. He has asked Dr. Renal if he can have a break form the dialysis to see how he goes. Dr. Renal agrees. He prepares a summary detailing the care he has provided to date for Mr. Everyman for Mr. Everyman's primary care physician, Dr. Patricia Primary, advising her of the temporary suspension in Mr. Everyman's dialysis.

Activities: Dr. Renal records the date when he commenced supervision of peritoneal dialysis for Mr. Everyman. Relevant information including problem lists, allergy lists, medication lists, progress notes, plans for care, are included in the summary. [Interaction: Suspend Care Provision]

Postcondition: The record summarizing the care provided for Mr. Adam Everyman is received by Dr. Primary and uploaded onto her clinical system.

Update Requirements Summary: The actors in this communication are Dr. Renal and Dr. Primary. The focus of the communication is Mr. Everyman's current record of care relating to his peritoneal dialysis provided by Dr. Renal, his status and plan of ongoing care.

5.1.3Emergency Encounter  (REPC_ST002001UV01)

Purpose

The purpose of this storyboard is to illustrate the Care Provision Separation Notification and Care Provision Requests for follow up specialty care. The example used here is a Physician Notification of Emergency Encounter Requiring Follow up Specialist Care.

Interaction List
Complete Care Provision REPC_IN004410UV01
Care Transfer Request REPC_IN002120UV01

 5.1.3.1Care Record Emergency Encounter (REPC_SN002001UV01)

Precondition: Mr. Adam Everyman is a patient who arrives at Good Health Hospital (GHH) emergency room via ambulance in respiratory distress and with an accelerated heart beat. He is seen by the physician on duty, Dr. Eric Emergency, who decides Mr. Everyman should be treated at this time. Mr. Everyman is checked-in for an ER visit by Mr. Christopher Clerk, the emergency room clerk, who retrieves Mr. Everyman's record from the GHH Patient Registry and creates the emergency check-in. Having administered nebulizer treatment to Mr. Everyman and stabilized him ready for discharge, Dr. Emergency sends an encounter summary to Mr. Everyman's primary care physician, Dr. Patricia Primary. In addition, Mr. Emergency advised Mr. Everyman that he should have a follow-up visit with a pulmonologist specialist and writes a referral request to Dr. Penny Puffer.

Activities: Adam Everyman arrives at GHH ER and is seen by Dr. Eric Emergency who stabilizes him and assesses when he is ready to be checked-out. Dr. Emergency writes a summary of the encounter and sends this to Dr. Patricia Primary, providing details of the assessment, treatment provided and follow up care required [Interaction: Complete Care Provision].

Dr. Emergency also sends a referral for Adam Everyman for a follow-up visit to Dr. Penny Puffer [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ].

Postcondition: The summary of the ER encounter for Adam Everyman is received by Dr. Primary and used to inform ongoing management of Adam Everyman. Dr. Primary notes a referral to Dr. Puffer has also been made and she makes a note to check with Dr. Puffer on the outcome of that follow up visit. The GHH ER clerk completes the checkout information for Mr. Everyman and checks him out of the GHH Emergency Room.

Update Requirements Summary: The primary actors for this communication are Dr. Emergency, Dr. Primary and Dr. Puffer. The foci of the communication are Dr. Emergency's referral request sent to Dr. Puffer and Adam Everyman's ER visit sent to both Drs. Primary and Puffer.

 5.1.4.1Care Record Hospital Discharge Summary (REPC_SN002002UV01)

Precondition: Mr. Adam Everyman is an inpatient at Good Health Hospital (GGH). His physician, Dr. Aaron Attend, refers him for thoracic surgery to Dr. Cutter who agrees to perform surgery on Mr. Everyman, adding him to his surgical list at GHH on June 27. Following the thoracic surgery to rule out lung cancer, Dr. Cutter considers that the surgical results indicate Mr. Everyman has lung cancer. He refers Mr. Everyman for a second opinion to Dr. Dunst, a pulmonologist, before proceeding with ongoing care by himself in the Gamma Cancer Clinic. Dr. Cutter also refers Mr. Everyman to the Quit Now for Smoking cessation education program. Dr. Cutter provides an event summary of the surgery to Dr. Attend, who also receives a copy of Mr. Everyman's visit to Quit Now.

Activities: Dr. Attend refers Mr. Everyman to Dr. Cutter with a request for thoracic surgery to rule out lung cancer. He includes a health summary as part of this request [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ]. Dr Cutter acknowledges this request and confirms he has added Mr Everyman to his surgical list for 27 June [Interaction:Care Transfer Promise].

Following the thoracic surgery on Mr. Everyman Dr. Cutter refers Mr. Everyman for a second opinion to Dr. Dunst. Dr. Cutter compiles a summary of Mr. Everyman's surgery along with the health summary provided by Dr. Attend and sends one referral request to Dr. Dunst and another to the Quit Now for Smoking cessation education program [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ].

Dr. Cutter also compiles the same summary of Mr. Everyman's surgery along with the proposed care plan and sends these to both the Hospital Surgical Review Board and Dr. Attend. This notification includes details of the procedures performed, the surgical findings and an adverse event that occurred during the procedure [Interaction: Complete Care Provision].

Following Mr. Everyman's visits to Quit Now, a report on these visits is sent by Quit Now to Dr. Primary for her records [Interaction: Activate Care Provision].

Postcondition: Having referred Mr. Everyman to Dr. Cutter, Dr. Attend receives a discharge summary report on the outcome of the surgical referral, including post surgical follow up treatment and care and ongoing progress reports from the Quit Now service.

Update Requirements Summary: The actors for this communication are Drs. Attend, Cutter, Dunst and the Quit Now clinic. The foci of the communication are Dr. Attend's health summary and referral request to Dr. Cutter, Dr. Cutter's referral request and inpatient surgical summary sent to Dr. Dunst and the Quit Now clinic, Dr. Cutter's inpatient surgical summary and proposed plan of care sent to Dr. Attend, and the Quit Now clinic visit summary sent to Dr. Attend.

Interaction List

 5.1.5.1Care Record Ongoing Specialist Care Provision (REPC_SN002003UV01)

Precondition: Dr. Patricia Primary, Primary Care Physician at Good Health Hospital (GHH) Outpatient Clinic, is seeing a 75-year old male patient Mr. Adam Everyman, complaining of pain in his hip joint. She refers Mr. Everyman to Dr. Joint, a rheumatologist at GHH Patient Ward, with a request for him to see and give advice regarding Mr. Everyman's' complaint. Dr. Joint is under no contract or legal obligation to see the patient. Dr. Joint agrees to see Mr. Everyman. Mr. Everyman has an initial consultation with Dr. Joint and subsequent three monthly followup meetings. Following each visit Dr. Joint sends Dr. Primary a progress report on Mr. Everyman.

Activities: Dr. Primary refers Mr. Everyman to Dr. Joint with a copy of Mr. Everyman's' clinical summary and relevant clinical history to date [Interaction: Care Transfer Request ]. Dr. Joint agrees to see Mr. Everyman and notifies Dr. Primary to that effect [Interaction: Care Transfer Promise ].

Once Dr. Joint has seen Mr. Everyman he sends Dr. Primary a report containing a summary of his exam and an outline of his intended ongoing care management [Interaction: Activate Care Provision]. Every subsequent three months Dr. Joint provides progress reports on Mr. Everyman to Dr. Primary [Interaction:Append Care Provision ].

Postcondition: Dr. Primary receives an initial and three monthly reports from Dr. Joint on Mr. Everyman's progress and takes the information contained therein into account in her ongoing treatment of Mr. Everyman's other health problems.

Update Requirements Summary: The actors for this communication are Dr. Primary and Dr. Joint. The foci of this communication are Dr. Primary's referral request to Dr. Joint, Dr. Joint's initial consultation summary plus Dr. Joint's subsequent examination summaries and ongoing plans of care sent to Dr. Primary.

 5.1.6.1Separation of Care (REPC_SN002004UV01)

Precondition: Dr. Primary, Primary Care Physician in Good Health Hospital (GHH) Outpatient Clinic, has been seeing a 75-year old male patient Mr. Everyman, with multiple health problems. Dr. Primary is closing her practice and is writing Care Provision summaries for use by the patient and providers whom the patient elects to see.

Activities: Dr. Primary records the date of onset of Care Provision by her practice and the date care ended in her practice. Update Requirements Summary: The actor for this communication is Dr. Primary. The focus of this communication is Mr. Everyman's record of care provided by Dr. Primary.

5.4.1Care Record  (REPC_RM004000UV01)

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   Care Provision (REPC_DM000000UV)
Care Record RMIM Overview

The Care Record RMIM captures the data and associations that are needed when the responsibility of Care Provision has been assumed and a record summary of the acts relevant to the care provision including any requested, proposed or activated care plans. This model, and its accompanying diagram, is very similar to the Care Provision DMIM but uses "local CMETs" to abstract the complexity of the care structures associated with the Care Provision Request Act. The reader should refer to the DMIM discussion before reviewing the RMIM followed by the discussion for each care structure. The discussion of this model will focus on features that do not exist in the DMIM, and were not directly addressed in the review of that model.

CareProvisionEvent Focal Class

As noted in the discussion of the DMIM a Care Provision Event records and summarizes the acts relevant whilst the performer is responsible for the care provision of the target of care.

Associations

The following associations are defined for a Care Record. Each association captures the relationship between the Care Provision Event and an entity or act that plays a significant part of the Care Record.

Participations

recordTarget: the record target is the entity for which the Care Record is being recorded. There can only be one record target for a Care Record. The target entity information is captured within the R_Patient CMET or R_CaredEntity local CMET. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

subject: the subject or subjects are the target(s) of the care provision being record when the subject is not the record target. The subject information is captured within the R_Patient CMET or R_CaredEntity local CMET. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

author: the party that produces the record of a Care Provision Event. This may be an assigned person or organization (R_AssignedParty) such as a healthcare provider, a related party (R_RelatedParty) such as a family member or a non-healthcare organization, or the patient (R_Patient) themselves. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

dataEnterer: the transcriptionist who enters the information of the Care Record. The data enterer information is captured within the R_AssignedPerson CMET.

verifier: the supervisor who verifies the Care Record. There is support for more than one level of verification. The verifying party information is captured within the R_AssignedPerson CMET.

performer: the parties who take part in actually carrying out the Care Provision Event being record. There may be multiple performers because several parties may collaborate in performance of Care Provision. These parties may be an assigned person or organization (R_AssignedParty) such as a healthcare provider, a related party (R_RelatedParty) such as a family member or a non-healthcare organization, or the patient (R_Patient) themselves. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

primaryInformationRecipient: the party who is intended to receive the Care Record notification. The recipient is usually the author of the associated care transfer request or the primary care provider. These may be represented by an assigned person or organization (R_AssignedParty), a related party (R_RelatedParty) such as a family member or court, or the patient (R_Patient) themselves. Refer to the respective CMETs for further description.

Act Relationships

inFullfilmentOf: this relationship links the Care Provision Event to the promise and/or request Act that it fulfills. Only the id attribute is provided to link the Act for which the Care Provision Event fulfills.

component: links the Care Provision Event to the care plan which is carried out as part of the Care Provision. Refer to A_CarePlan in the Care Structures topic for further description this local CMET.

pertinentInformation1: links the Care Provision Event to the care plan which is informative in the context of the care provision but not directly relevant to this Care Record. Refer to A_CarePlan in the Care Structures topic for further description this local CMET.

pertinentInformation3: links the Care Provision Event to a set of pertinent clinical statements provided as part of the information supplied in the Care Record. Refer to A_CareStatement in the Care Structures topic for further description this local CMET.

reason: a relationship to a concern recorded as the primary reason for the Care Provision. A problem, not specifically a diagnosis, which is the primary reason for the Care Provision can also be presented using this relationship. Refer to A_ConcernTracking in the Care Structures topic for further description this local CMET.

pertinentInformation2: a Care Record has a set of lists containing various Clinical Statements as relevant information, which is ongoing and not necessarily captured as part of the Care Provision Event. It may include a problem list, current medication list or a list of risk factors. Refer to A_StatementCollector in the Care Structures topic for further description this local CMET.

Reference: the Care Event or Care Events (encounter-based, episode-based, or general care-based collections) that the Care Record relates to, represented by the A_CareEvent CMET

ReplacementOf: a link to a replaced Care Record. The Care Provision Event being the target of this act relationship has a status of obsolete and can either contain just the instance identifier of the replaced Care Record or the entire Care Record information.
