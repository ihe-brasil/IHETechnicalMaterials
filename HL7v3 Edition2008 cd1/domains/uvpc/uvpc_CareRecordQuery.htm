6.1.1Aged Care Assessment Record Query  (REPC_ST002201UV01)

Purpose
This storyboard demonstrates querying a state or national database of eligibility for aged care assessment records to establish currency (expiry date), scope (respite, permanent, high care/low care, community, residential etc) of an existing eligibility record; which aged care assessment providers undertook the eligibility assessment; and whether any expired eligibility assessment record for the same individual exists.

 6.1.1.1Aged Care Assessment Record Query (REPC_SN002201UV01)

Precondition:

Nancy Nightingale is evaluating whether she can provide a package of community based services to potential client Mrs. Eve Everywoman. However, it is not clear from the application form (completed on her behalf by her husband, Boris Betterhalf) whether Mrs. Everywoman has been assessed as eligible for this subsidized service. As she is authorized to access the national aged care eligibility assessment forms registry and has Mr. Betterhalf's consent, Nancy Nightingale sends a request to the national registry to determine whether a current or previous aged care eligibility assessment form exists for Mrs. Everywoman.

Activities:

Nancy Nightingale logs onto the national aged care eligibility assessment registry. She enters Mrs. Everywoman's details to query whether a current or previous eligibility assessment exists for Mrs. Everywoman. She receives a response confirming that an eligibility assessment was completed two years previously, is now expired, and was undertaken by an assessment team in Melbourne, where Mrs. Everywoman used to live.

Postcondition:

Nancy Nightingale notifies Mrs. Everywoman that she requires a current eligibility assessment before she can process her application for community based services. She advises Mrs. Everywoman she will make arrangements for her to be seen by an eligibility assessment team who will be able to re-assess her care needs and eligibility for government subsidized services.

Updates Requirement Summary:

The communicators of this query message, Nancy Nightingale and the national registry system, ensure ready access by clinicians to copies of current or preexisting aged care eligibility assessment records, avoiding delays in accessing services due to expired or non existent assessment records.

 6.1.2.1Patient Populates Personal Health Record (REPC_SN002202UV01)

Precondition: Mr. Adam Everyman wishes to populate a personal health record with information on his condition from Dr. Patricia Primary held in her clinical system.

Activities: Mr. Adam Everyman requests specific information relating to his cardiac condition from Dr. Patricia Primary [Interaction: Get Care Record Profile Query]. Dr. Primary responds by sending the answers to the specific information requested to Mr. Everyman who is able to incorporate the information into his own personal health record [Interaction: Get Care Record Profile Response]. Mr Everyman uploads the information on his cardiac condition into his personal health record [Interaction: Activate Care Provision].

Alternate flow: Mr Everyman requests copies of particular test results and treatments relating to his cardiac condition from Dr Primary [Interaction: Get Care Record Profile Query]. Dr Primary responds by sending the requested documents to Mr Everyman .[Interaction: Get Care Record Profile Response]

Postcondition: Mr. Everyman now has all the documents and information he requires on his cardiac condition from Dr. Primary.

Updates Requirement Summary: The communicators of this storyboard, Mr. Everyman and Dr Primary, are focused on the sourcing and exchange of extracts of Mr. Everyman's patient record.

Note: This storyboard is supported under the Care Record Query Topic and the Care Record Document Topic. Care Record Summary document is the document type that would be returned by Physician A.

6.4.1Find Care Record Candidate Query  (QUPC_RM040100UV01)

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   None Specified

The Find Care Record Candidate Query request RMIM provides the list of query parameters needed to retrieve a list of summarized Care Records based on the specified criteria. These query parameters are as follows:

  • PatientId: The unique identifier (II) of the patient whose records are being retrieved. The parameter is mandatory and should contain exactly one patient identifer, which must not be null.
  • PatientName: The label (PN) by which the patient whose records are being retrieved is known and communicated with. The patient name is used for verification of the patient identifier for the query request. The parameter is required to be supported but has a maximum of just one patient name.
  • PatientAdministrativeGender: Indicates the gender (CE) of the patient whose records are being retrieved. Used to assist in identification of the patient accurately, in the context of other parameters. The parameter is required to be supported but has a maximum of just one gender code value.
  • PatientBirthTime: The calendar date (TS) on which a patient whose records are being retrieved was born. Used to assist in identification of the patient accurately, in the context of other parameters. The parameter is required to be supported but has a maximum of just one date value.
  • CareRecordTimePeriod: The date range (IVL<TS>) of Care Record contributions to be included in the candidate list. A query can request new contribution that have been processed for this patient since the last query request. For example, a system can query about a patient each month and only get the new contributions to the patient's health record. The parameter is required to be supported but has a maximum of just one date range.
  • CareProvisionCode: The code (CD) representing the type of the Care Records to be retrieved. A query can request a list of Care Records of a specific type, such as a hospital discharge summary, or a more general grouping of Care Record types, such as ambulatory patient visits. The parameter is required to be supported but has a maximum of just one type of Care Record code value.
  • CareProvisionReason: The code (CD) representing the condition that is the "reason" for the Care Provision reported in the Care Record. A query can request a list of Care Records that are related to managing a current or previous condition, such as Pregnancy. The parameter is required to be supported allows multiple care provision reason code values.
  • MaximumCandidates: The maximum number (INT) of candidate Care Records to be returned that match the query criteria. For example 15= fifteen most recent Care Records that match the query criteria, unspecified= all Care Records that match the query criteria. The default is for all Care Records to be returned. The parameter is required to be supported but has a maximum of just one integer value.

6.4.2Care Record Query  (QUPC_RM040000UV01)

Diagram


Click thumbnail above to open larger graphic in a new window
Description
Parent:   None Specified

The Care Record Query request RMIM provides the list of query parameters needed to retreive a specific Care Record determined by its unique identifier. These query parameters are as follows:

  • CareRecordId: The unique identifier (II) of the Care Record to be retrieved. The parameter is mandatory and should contain exactly one Care Record identifier which must not be null.
  • PatientId: The unique identifier (II) of the patient whose record is being retrieved. The parameter is required to be supported but has a maximum of just one patient identifier. This parameter can be provided to ensure that Care Record being requested is for the correct patient or when Care Record identfiers are only unique for a particular patient.
  • VersionTimestamp: The date/time (TS) at which point the state of the Care Record retrieved should be reflected, that is, retreive the Care Record snapshot at that point in time. The parameter is required to be supported but has a maximum of just one timestamp.
  • IncludeCarePlanAttachment: An indicator (BL) that, if set to true, would include any care plan attachments that are available. If set to false, care plan attachments would not be sent or returned in the query response. The default is 'false'. The parameter is required to be supported but has a maximum of just one boolean indicator.
  • MaximumHistoryStatements: The number (INT) of repetitions of each type of historical clinical statement (e.g. A1Cs, weight, dispensed medications, etc.) to be returned. For example 1= most recent, 2= last two clinical statements, unspecified= all available clinical statements. The default is for all records to be returned. The parameter is required to be supported but has a maximum of just one integer value.
