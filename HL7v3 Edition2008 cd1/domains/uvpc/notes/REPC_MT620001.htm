Professional Service

(ProcedureEvent)

Description

This is the information that is recorded and maintained on a consultative service or surgical or physical service (procedure) provided to a patient.

Rationale

It is necessary to know of clinical and non-clinical services which have occurred in order to aid in interpretation of results. Counselling, education, surgeries and physical therapy are examples of the types of services that can be captured.

Constraints

Annotation is only permitted if Annotation Indicator is not present and vice versa

Code Domain

ActUncertainty

 

 

 

Description

Used to specifically flag the record as suspect or uncertain. The default is "no assertion of uncertainty". This does not mean that the record is unquestionable, but rather that the provider recording the record has no reason to question its accuracy. An assertion of "uncertainty" indicates that while there is a high enough chance of the record being accurate to warrant its presence in the EHR, there is reason to suspect that some or all of it may be inaccurate. Additional details about the reasons for the uncertainty may be conveyed in notes.

Rationale

Known uncertainty is important to convey to other clinicians and will affect how the record is interpreted.

The attribute is optional because for systems that support SNOMED, this concept will be communicated as part of the Type. For systems which choose to support the element, it should always be present with a non-null value because it should always be known whether uncertainty is being asserted or not.

Code Domain

x_VeryBasicConfidentialityKind

 

 

 

Description

Communicates the desire of the patient to restrict access to this record. Provides support for additional confidentiality constraint, giving patients a level of control over their information. Methods for accessing masked event records will be governed by each jurisdiction (e.g. court orders, shared secret/consent, etc.).

Can also be used to communicate that the information is deemed to be sensitive and should not be communicated or exposed to the patient (at least without the guidance of the authoring or other responsible healthcare provider).

Valid values are: 'normal' (denotes 'Not Masked'); 'restricted' (denotes 'Masked') and 'taboo' (denotes 'patient restricted'). The default is 'normal' signifying 'Not Masked'. Either or both of the other codes can be asserted to indicate masking by the patient from providers or masking by a provider from the patient, respectively.

Rationale

Allows the patient to have discrete control over access to their health data. Awareness of the patient's desire for confidentiality is important even for providers who have access to the data because it indicates a need for extra caution before disclosing the information to other care providers.

Also allows providers to mark records as "not for patient display" for circumstances where the patient has access to their own EHR. (There are some pieces of information where it would be detrimental to a patient's health or ongoing care were they to be aware of those records without careful guidance.)

Two repetitions are supported to allow indicating that the record is masked from both a provider and a patient perspective.

The attribute is optional because not all systems will support masking.

Implementation

The value specified for a particular record may be overridden by a higher level masking applied to an indication, a type of data or even all patient records.

Code Domain

ActPhysicalProcedureCategoryList

 

 

 

Description

Describes the categorization of the service. E.g. Psychological Counseling, Smoking Cessation, Cardiac Surgeries, etc.

Rationale

Allows categorizing professional services for presentation. A given person may have had numerous procedures related to a particular area. By associating categories with procedures, a person viewing the EHR information can first look at a list of categories and then drill down to the specific procedures.

The presence of this field is essential to prevent users from being overwhelmed, however not all services will necessarily be categorizable. Therefore this element is marked as 'populated'.

Description

When set to true, specifically flags the record as "did not occur". The default is false. Additional details about the reasons for refuting the record may be conveyed in notes.

Rationale

This is primarily used to supercede records where an assertion was made which is subsequently determined to be false. It is important to be able to make explicit statements that something is known to not be true.

The attribute is optional because for systems that support SNOMED, this concept will be communicated as part of the Type. For systems which choose to support the element explicitly, it should always be present with a non-null value because it should always be known whether the record is being refuted or not.

Description

Identifies the person(s) who actually performed the action described in the record.

Rationale

The person who makes the decision to capture information in the electronic record is not always the person who actually performed the action. Knowledge of the performer is useful both for tracking responsibility and for subsequent follow-up.

Multiple repetitions are allowed for those circumstances where more than one provider is involved.

Implementation

If not specified, it is assumed that the author is also the performer. I.e. If the author and performer are the same, only the author should be sent. If the author is not the performer, but the performer is not known, a null flavor of UNK (unknown) should be used.

Care Compositions

(A_CareEventIdentified.PatientCareProvisionEvent)

Description

A care composition is a record, which summarizes the events that happened during care including who is responsible for the care provided. Examples include encounters, episodes and general "care events" such as "gynecological care".

Rationale

Allows linking records to encounters, episodes and other care compositions. Useful for searching and navigation of the patient's record.

This element incorporates the model:

A_CareEventIdentified

Notes

(A_AnnotationUniversal.Annotation)

Description

This is a list of comments made about the common observation by providers. Information captured here includes the provider making the comments; and excludes information that would render the allergy record invalid. This information will only be seen when another provider reviews the common observation. Urgent or targeted messages should be sent using a different mechanism (e.g. phone).

Rationale

Allows various Providers to attach comments to an existing common observation record, and thus improving cross-provider communications.
