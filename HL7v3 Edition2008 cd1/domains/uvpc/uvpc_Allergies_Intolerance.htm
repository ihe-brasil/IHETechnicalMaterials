Introduction
Scope and Use Cases

Allergy and Intolerance observations are some of the most commonly recorded, reviewed and transmitted information in the medical record. Clinicians record allergies and intolerances as a way to remind themselves and other providers to avoid giving substances that are known to potentially cause harm or discomfort to the patient. The process of identifying known adverse reactions and the believed causes for them is a critical element of good clinical practice and is essential for safety and quality (Nehta). Within the context of allergies, adverse reactions are harmful or undesirable responses after administration of an agent/substance. Intolerance is a condition where the patient has been noted to have an adverse reaction and a judgment is made that the reaction was caused by a given substance. Administration of substances (in the form of ingestion, inhalation, injection, or direct contact) may result in adverse reactions which may be classified as severe or mild, occur after variable lengths of time, and the relationship between exposure and the adverse reaction may be uncertain. Additionally, the noted exposure may be (usually is) to a mixture of complex substances. Additionally, many substances cross react, so that it may be a class of medications that cause problems. Therefore the assignment of causality of the actual relationship between the offending substance and the reaction is generally always uncertain to some degree.

Use Case 1: The clinical "Allergy" list: Many make the distinction between a true allergy and intolerance. A true allergy is a type of intolerance with where the patient's immune system "hyper" reacts after exposure to that agent/substance or a chemically similar substance. True allergies typically result in a rash or swelling and may be life threatening. They also have defined treatments such as steroids antihistamines, or epinephrine. The classic example of this is the typical penicillin allergy. Pragmatically, both true allergies and intolerances are recorded in a separate section of the medical record. Although this section is often (usually) called "the allergy list" it more accurately represents a list of "intolerances" or "alerts" since it mixes true allergies and intolerances. Different providers will have varying opinions on the need to specifically note minor intolerances to medications such as gastric upset in this section, however there is general agreement that true allergies and severe intolerances be recorded in this section and reviewed regularly. Some severe intolerances such as heparin induced thrombocytopenia do not follow the same physiologic pathways as true allergy but follow the same need for explicit and clear warning to prescribing or administrating providers and therefore are grouped in with "allergies." Fortunately, the modeling for allergies and intolerances for this purpose does not need to include details of pathogenesis to this degree, so this mixing while technically inaccurate is of little consequence. Logically, an allergy or intolerance is a type of medical condition or concern. It is an observation (as judged by the clinician) that asserts the patient does or does not have a condition where a substance administration is felt to be associated with an adverse reaction. It may tracked like other medical concerns as a series of observations as further clarification or understanding of the nature of that allergy occurs. For example, an observation of an allergy to percocet may be refined to a mild intolerance to "oxycodone", but tracked separately from the concern about allergy beta-lactams being refined to exclude cephalosporins. However, there are important distinctions between most medical concerns and allergies concerns. Because they are used as specific reminders not to administer or to alter therapy, they are generally called out as a separately tracked list. Other clinical conditions and patient data may also alter therapy as well (for example dose changes based on age or renal function, or contraindications to medications in certain medical conditions) but allergies are differentiated because they are recorded as the type of therapy to avoid rather than as conditions which usually can't be avoided. Also, in many cases systems may require explicit review and specific negative assertions for various types of known allergens. For example, it is most common to see a section of a document or element of the EMR which explicitly states that the patient does not have any known allergies or any known drug allergies. The degree of granularity for specific negation is variable from specific application to application or from use case to use case. For example, many systems record explicitly that the patient is NOT allergic to Latex or IV contrast. In general, obviously harmful substance administrations such as poisoning or overdoses are not included in allergy/intolerance lists because there should never be such an intent to treat the patient in such a manor. Similarly, seasonal or pet allergies which share many of the same underlying pathophysiology as true allergy are typically not recorded in allergy lists because there is generally not concern that these substances will be administered as therapy planning. This is not absolute, however as some physicians do prefer to track environmental allergies in this section. Food allergies on the other hand, generally are included in this list since diets may be ordered or prescribed and many medications or immunizations may include food substances, however. Most notably many immunizations are grown in eggs.

Data Capture: Allergies/Intolerance lists are typically reviewed at many points of patient care. For example, physicians typically review patient allergies when they first see a patient or perform History and Physicals. Nurses typically have a similar intake assessment where allergies are reviewed as do pharmacists. Typical use patterns typically involve a review rather than capture of new allergies because allergy information is considered to be important by a wide variety of people and so it is frequent that some records are available which provide this information. For example, a nurse may review a list of the patients allergies from transfer records when he/she does their intake assessment, and a physician may review the nurses list with the patient when he/she does their history and physical. It is also common that allergy lists are reviewed and updated in the course of care when new information is available. For example, it may be noted that the patient has a new suspect allergy when noted to have an adverse reaction after exposure to a medication. Similarly there is often review and correction a result of need to challenge the certainty that the allergy exists. For example an allergy to Penicillin may be noted. This may persist for quite some time before the patient actually needs antibiotics, and further questioning may reveal that the reaction happened many years ago and that patient is unsure about specifically what happened. Cautious trials of therapy or even further verification testing may proceed as a result. Additional clarification details may be appended such as "patient has tolerated cephalosporins in the past" (i.e. cross reactivity has been ruled out) or the allergy may even be marked as erroneous. Allergies and some intolerances may be supported by evidence such as allergy sensitivity testing or serology tests.

Data Use: Allergy information is useful for a variety of use cases. First, and importantly, it is used in the simple human review of allergy information. Patients may forget or may be unable to indicate allergy information. Indeed, even the same provider may have difficulty remembering a patients allergies as the patient moves through the continuity of care. As a result, allergy information is usually provided from provider to provider on any patient transfer of care, discharge, or referral. This occurs through paper documents, simple storage in an EMR where both clinicians share the same EMR, or exchange between clinical information systems when the clinicians use different systems. Electronic transfer of this information may be either through structured documents, messages, or queries depending on particular use cases and or system capabilities. Allergy data always includes some representation of the allergen, however also should ideally include the reaction with or without severity. It may include additional details including when the event occurred or supporting details. The data capture is often incomplete, however, and even the allergen may be at a hazy level of granularity. For example, the patient may only remember that they are allergic to a "-cillins" (several different types of antibiotics) Allergy information needs to be codified and computer processable since many systems use and exchange allergy data for the expressed purpose of drug order to allergy checks, and may require allergy information to be assessed. Many systems also require statements about the status allergy review within a given time period, thus it is important to include even updates even when the assertion is simply to repeat that no allergies are known to exist. In some cases allergy information is used for reporting to external agencies.

Walk-Through

This is a walk through of a generic allergy/intolerance model, which can be constrained to record and list a patient's allergy or intolerances to various levels of agent/substance hierarchy, as well as to more generic concepts such as food and the environment. The model depicts the three major components of the Allergy/Intolerance structure, namely:

  • Allergy/Intolerance List
  • Allergy/Intolerance Summary Observation
  • Allergy/Intolerance Concern

These component parts of the allergy/intolerance structure will be described in detail here, beginning with the core Act classes. The definitions for class attributes are available in Reference Information Model (RIM) documentation and will not be repeated here.

Allergy/Intolerance Core Act Classes

AllergyIntoleranceList - REPC_RM000322UV

This RMIM is used to create a list from allergy/intolerance detail observation (tracked as allergy concerns) and allergy/intolerance summary observations. Different providers may use this RMIM to create a list of allergies and intolerances for different custodians/owners.

Attributes

Code (Allergy/Intolerance Type): This is a mandatory code that lists the allergy/intolerance(s) and is constructed from allergy/intolerance concerns and allergy/intolerance summary.

Participations

Informant (Provider or Patient or Related Party): In the case where the information in the record is being captured second-hand, this identifies the source of the information. Understanding the source of data is essential to its interpretation. If not specified, it is assumed that the author is capturing information of which they have first-hand awareness.

recordTarget1 (Patient): This identifies the patient associated with the allergy/intolerance record.

author1 (Provider): This identifies the provider who is responsible for the decision to capture the Allergy/Intolerance in the patient's EHR. Used to identify responsibility for accuracy and relevance of the information.

responsibleParty (Provider): Identity of the provider who is taking responsibility for the actions of the author, usually a supervisor. Allows for the trail of responsibility for the action.

location: Indicates the service delivery location where the allergy was recorded. Indicates where records are likely kept for follow-up. May also be useful in understanding the context in which the allergy/intolerance was recorded.

custodian: Constitutes the owner of the list.

Act Relationships

component: Has 'component' relationship to the AllergyIntolerancConcern class, indicating that the allergy/intolerance concerns do contribute to the creation of the allergy/intolerance list.Component1: Has 'component' relationship to the AllergyIntolerancSummaryObservation class, indicating that the allergy/intolerance summary observations contribute to the creation of the allergy/intolerance list.

AllergyIntoleranceSummaryObservation - REPC_MT000323UV

This class is employed by a provider to assert that a patient has no known allergy or intolerance whatsoever, or that the patient has no known allergy or intolerance to any generic concept of an agent/substance such as Drug, Food, etc.

This recording of 'No Known Indicator' is not a derivation from the absence of allergy/intolerance concern; instead, it is recorded as an explicit assertion based on the provider making the necessary assessment. It is about the fact that the list contains no concerns of a particular type.

The only reason for needing this class, is to be able to assert a 'No Known ...' observation without tying it to a 'Concern', i.e. so as not to raise a concern just to be able to say that there is no concern.

Attributes

code (Allergy or Intolerance): This is a mandatory that indicates that the summary assertion is in reference to an allergy or intolerance.

negationInd (No Known Indicator): This is a mandatory attribute whose value is fixed to 'true', indicating that this class is used to make negative assertions (assertion of non-existence of an allergy/intolerance concern) only.

value (Type of Allergy or Intolerance Summary): This is a mandatory whose domain indicates the generic class of concepts for which negative assertion is being made. Include the codes for Allergy (negated as: No Known Allergy), Intolerance (negated as: No Known Intolerances), Drug (negated as: No Known Drug Allergy), Food (negated as: No Known Food Allergy), etc

Participations

Participants in this class are: author (Provider), responsibleParty (Supervisor), subject2 (Patient), informant (Provider, or Patient, or Related Party), participant, and location. For full descriptions of these participants, please refer to the 'Participations' section for the AllergyIntoleranceList class.

Act Relationships

The only actRelationship from this class is to serve as a source for allergy/intolerance problem list as compiled by the AllergyIntoleranceList class.

AllergyIntoleranceConcern - REPC_RM000324UV

This class is used to track a patient's allergy or intolerance to a specific agent or substance, as observed by various providers at various points in time and locations. The name and nature of the concern is derived from the first allergy/intolerance observation made for the agent/substance, which could be an indication of a negative assertion for an allergy or intolerance to an agent/substance (e.g. Patient is not allergic to penicillin).

Attributes

Id (Concern Identifier): A mandatory attribute for uniquely identifying the particular allergy/intolerance concern. Allows on-going observations made on the same initial problem to be tracked as the same concern.

statusCode (Concern Status): Used to summarize the status of the specific allergy/intolerance as indicated by the last observation made on the allergy or intolerance. For full description of the various statuses of a Concern, please refer to the Concern Tracker CMET: REPC_RM000300UV A_ConcernTracking

effectiveTime (Time concern started and ended): Used to summarize the period of the concern, based on the information contained in the first and last allergy/intolerance observations made.

confidentialityCode: Used to assign privacy to the particular allergy/intolerance concern.

Participations

Participants in this class are: author (Provider), responsibleParty (Supervisor), subject2 (Patient), informant (Provider, or Patient, or Related Party), participant, and location. For full descriptions of these participants, please refer to the 'Participations' section for the AllergyIntoleranceList class.

Act Relationships

subject: Has 'subject' relationship to the AllergyIntoleranceObservation class, indicating that the allergy concern is the subject of one or more allergy or intolerance observations.

For full descriptions of the actRelationships, please refer to the 'Act Relationship' section for the generic PC Concern Tracking CMET: REPC_RM000300UV - A_ConcernTracking. The four main actRelationships of interest are employed to achieve the following:

  • replacing an existing concern with a new one
  • linking a concern to another concern
  • making a concern a sub-concern of another concern
  • specifying an existing in various sequel relationships to another concern.

Other classes

The following is a brief description of other classes that are defined as part of the AllergyIntoleranceConcern structure:

ControlActEvent: This class provides the ability to manage/indicate the state transitions of an allergy/intolerance concern, including the ability to indicate 'resolution' (i.e. the allergy or intolerance had been resolved, and was no longer a concern).

AllergyIntoleranceDetailObservation

The AllergyIntoleranceDetailObservation structure comprises a collection of classes including Observation, Substance Administration, and Control Acts. It also includes modeling of materials and numerous Act Relationships. These will be described in detail here, beginning with the core Act classes. The definitions for class attributes are available in Reference Information Model (RIM) documentation and will not be repeated here.

Attributes

Id (Allergy or Intolerance Identifier): Identifies unique occurrence of an allergy or intolerance observation record.

code (Allergy or Intolerance): Indicates that the object of the observation is to record an allergy or an intolerance concern in the patient's EHR.

negationInd: Used to assert that a patient is, or is not allergic/intolerant to the agent in question. The negationInd attribute is set to 'false' when recording that a patient has allergy or intolerance to the agent/substance in question; whereas it is set to 'true' when asserting that a patient has no allergy or intolerance to the agent/substance.

statusCode: Has fixed status of 'completed', indicating a point in time observation that is not updateable.

value (Type of Allergy/Intolerance): This has either a coded value or a null flavor of 'N/A'. If recording as a code/value, the code should have the semantic of 'allergy to xxx'. If recording the allergy to an agent through the 'participant', then the value should have a null flavor.

methodCode: Indicates the provider's overall causality assessment and cognitive procedure for asserting that the patient is, or is not allergic or intolerant to the agent in question.

Participations

participant: Identifies the exposed agent (medication or a generic administrable material) that is believed to have triggered the allergy or intolerance. This participation must not be specified if the value attribute of the focal class has a valid code with appropriate semantic. On the other hand, this participation must be specified if the value attribute of the focal class has a null flavor of 'N/A'

Other participants in this class are: author (Provider), responsibleParty (Supervisor), subject2 (Patient), informant (Provider, or Patient, or Related Party), participant, and location. For full descriptions of these other participants, please refer to the 'Participations' section for the AllergyIntoleranceList class.

Act Relationships

subject: Indicates a particular type of reaction experienced by the patient which provides evidence for the existence (or non-existence) of the allergy/intolerance. May help ascertain the severity of the condition, and provide direction for management.

subjectOf2: Indicates the gravity of the patient's allergy or intolerance to the agent or the gravity of the patient's reaction to the agent.

subjectOf1: Indicates the various comments that providers have recorded about this allergy/intolerance.

support: Points to result of a previously conducted allergy test that supports or refutes the allergy/intolerance observation. Provides evidence for recording the allergy or intolerance.

Other Classes

The following is a brief description of other classes that are defined as part of the AllergyIntoleranceObservation structure:

Reaction: This is a recording of a patient reaction that is believed to be associated with the allergy/intolerance. This aids providers in distinguishing between proper allergies and non-allergy intolerances, and allows the provider recording the allergy/intolerance to assign appropriate severity to the allergy/allergy record.

SubstanceAdministration: Incidence of the patient's exposure to some substance (medication, food, etc) through administration that might have caused the reaction.

SeverityObservation: Indicates the gravity of the allergy, intolerance or reaction in terms of its actual or potential impact on the patient. Allows for sorting of allergy records. May influence whether contraindications must be managed.

Annotation: This is a list of comments made about the record by providers. Information captured here includes the provider making the comments; and excludes information that would render the record invalid. Allows various Providers to attach comments to an existing record, and thus improving cross-provider communications.

AllergyTestEvent: Indicates the specific allergy test that supports the recording of the allergy/intolerance. Provides evidence for recording the allergy/intolerance in the patient's EHR.

8.1.1Patient Report Food Allergy  (REPC_ST006006UV01)

Purpose

The purpose of this storyboard is to illustrate the recording of a food allergy and the need to harmonize with CDA.

 8.1.1.1Patient Report Food Allergy (REPC_SN006006UV01)

Precondition: On evaluation in Dr. Cultures office, Mr. Everyman is queried about the rash, and it is found that the rash occurred after eating peanut oil, which Mr. Everyman forgot to report as a known childhood allergy.

Activities: Dr. Culture updates the allergy list to reflect the food allergy to peanut oil. He references the previously recorded rash as the reaction and records this information in a CDA consult note back to Dr. Primary. Dr. Primary imports the allergy data from the note into his EMR.

Post-condition: The food allergy is recorded.

8.1.2Physician Adds Allergy Information  (REPC_ST006000UV01)

Purpose

The purpose of this storyboard is to illustrate a physician adding Allergy information to a patient record. This information is shared with other providers.

 8.1.2.1Physician Adds Allergy Information (REPC_SN006000UV01)
Precondition: Patient Adam Everyman is seeing Dr. Patricia Primary for evaluation of a cough. In the process of recording Mr. Everyman's history Dr. Primary states a history of no known drug allergies (NKDA). After reviewing Mr. Everyman's history and exam findings, Dr. Primary decides that Mr. Everyman probably has chronic bronchitis and should be referred to the pulmonologist Dr. Bob Breathright for further evaluation.

Activities: Dr Primary creates an electronic referral letter which includes the allergy information embedded in the document. He sends this data to Dr. Breathright as part of the electronic referral documents. Alternative flows include Dr. Primary posts documents or the allergy information to a central repository and Dr. Breathright queries the repository. Other flows might have Dr. Breathright query Dr. Primary's EMR directly.

PostConditions: Dr. Breathright accepts the referral and has the relevant allergy information.

8.1.3Physician Annotates Allergy  (REPC_ST006004UV01)

Purpose

The purpose of this storyboard is to show the need to annotate allergies with further test results (e.g. with skin testing) as evidence, and to correct prior allergy assumptions, where the allergy is refuted.

 8.1.3.1Physician Annotates Allergy (REPC_SN006004UV01)

Precondition: Mr. Everyman is subsequently noted to have Endocarditis with cultures growing Enterococcus. Dr. Ida D. Culture, the Infectious Disease consultant is called onto the case. Dr. Culture suggests that Penicillin skin testing be performed since it is much more effective than alternative agents.

Activities: Skin testing is performed which shows that Mr. Everyman is NOT allergic to penicillin after all. Dr. Culture records the correction to the allergy list. The updated information is shared with the pharmacy system.

Post-condition: The allergy list reflects no known drug allergies (NKDA) and the penicillin allergy is explicitly negated.

8.1.4Physician Clarifies Allergy Information  (REPC_ST006003UV01)

Purpose
The purpose of this storyboard is to clarify allergy information and the use of allergy information for drug/allergy checking. Allergens may cross react, therefore allergy information may require annotations that clarify the nature and scope of the allergy. Furthermore updates to allergy information is synchronized between multiple hospital systems.

 8.1.4.1Physician Clarifies Allergy Information (REPC_SN006003UV01)

Preconditions: Mr. Everyman has been admitted to the hospital and has had a single dose of antibiotics in the emergency room. Dr. Primary has written for Levofloxicin as an alternative to the standard formulary choice of Cefuroxime. Cefuroxime is a type of Cephalosporin. Cephalosporins generally have about a 10% cross reactivity with Penicillin. Culture results later show that the organism in Mr. Everyman's sputum sample are growing Staphalococcus which is resistant to Levofloxicin, but sensitive to Cefuroxime. Dr. Primary asks Mr. Everyman more about the nature of his penicillin allergy. Mr. Everyman admits that he has had other Cephalosporins in the past without any problem.

Activities: Dr. Primary discontinues the order for Levofloxicin, and adds an order for Cefuroxime. The system generates an alert indicating that there is a known cross reactivity between Cefuroxime and penicillin. Dr. Primary overrides the alert and adds an annotation about the fact that Mr. Everyman has tolerated other cephalosporins in the past without difficulty. The updated allergy information is transmitted to the pharmacy system. On reviewing the order, Dr. Drew Drugger, the pharmacist sees the annotation about cephalosporins.

Post condition: The system places the order, and also updates the pharmacy system. The allergies in both systems are updated to reflect the comment that cephalosporins are OK.

8.1.5Physician Enters Suspect Environmental Allergy  (REPC_ST006007UV01)

Purpose

The purpose of this storyboard is to demonstrate the linking of information in assessing a response to an allergy, and highlights the need to record the level of certainty, and reuse allergy information in related parts of a modular EMR such as a separate documentation solution. This storyboard refers to Use Case 2.

 8.1.5.1Physician Enters Suspect Environmental Allergy (REPC_SN006007UV01)

Precondition: Dr. Primary is viewing the EMR of Patient Everyman. Mr. Everyman has come to clinic to have a rash evaluated. The allergy list records the peanut oil food allergy but "NKDA" (no known drug allergies) and "NKEA" (no known environmental allergies).

Activities: Dr. Primary elicits the patient history. The patient has visited an Indian restaurant for the first time and was exposed to many new spices. The patient wonders whether the rash is due to the spices. Dr. Primary examines the rash and notices the typical linear pattern associated with a plant exposure. She questions the patient further and finds the patient had been working in the garden earlier in the week. The patient has never had cedar wood chips. Dr. Primary assures the patient that allergy to Indian spices is unlikely to be the cause of this rash. Rather, Dr. Primary identifies probable cedar wood chips and educates the patient on the disease. She places the problem "rash - probably due to cedar wood chips" on his problem list and gives the patient a prescription for Prednisone. She adds cedar wood chips to the allergy list with a certainty level of "probable" linking the problem as evidence. Dr. Primary dictates a note of the event. The transcription system accepts a feed including the allergy data.

Post-condition: Mr. Everyman has progress notes, findings, impressions, care plans, and a populated allergy list in the EMR that are captured in a manner that allows navigation from one related data item to the next. Specifically, the user is able to navigate from the current name of the allergy on the allergy list to the string of observations and treatment activities associated with management of that allergy over time. Additionally, the user is able to navigate from a data item to the allergy management programs supported by the data element.

8.1.6Physician Notes Severe Intolerance  (REPC_ST006008UV01)

Purpose

The purpose of this storyboard is to demonstrate the flow of information in tracking an allergy and highlighting the need to record reaction severity.

 8.1.6.1Physician Notes Severe Intolerance (REPC_SN006008UV01)

Precondition: Mr. Everyman is bitten by a bee in his back yard. A few hours later he notices that his face and lips are swollen. He feels generally unwell so proceeds to the Emergency room. In the emergency room he develops low blood pressure and shortness of breath. Dr. Eric Rescue, the emergency room physician diagnoses anaphylaxis due to a bee sting, and begins appropriate treatment.

Activities: Dr. Rescue records the problem "Anaphylaxis secondary to Bee sting" in the Problem List and "Bee's" in the allergy list, with a reaction of "anaphylaxis", and a severity of "severe"

Post-Conditions: Dr. Primary follows up with the patient to track problem of Bee allergy tracked on his problem list and prescribes an epinephrine pen, and also keeps the bee allergy on the allergy list.

8.1.7Physician Reviews Verifies Allergy Information  (REPC_ST006002UV01)

Purpose

The purpose of this storyboard is to illustrate the communication flow in reviewing and confirming allergy information, ensuring the hospital information system and pharmacy system has correct and updated information that is synchronized.

 8.1.7.1Physician Reviews Verifies Allergy Information (REPC_SN006002UV01)

Precondition: Mr. Everyman calls Dr. Primary with complaint that he has a fever and is coughing copious amounts of sputum, mostly green in color but sometimes including streaks of blood. Dr. Primary advises Mr. Everyman to go to the Emergency Room for evaluation.

Activities: Dr. Primary prepares an emergency department referral which includes allergy information as it's recorded in his EMR. In the emergency room, Nurse Carrie Careful reviews allergies with Mr. Everyman. Mr. Everyman is coughing severely and finds it hard to speak without coughing. The history goes a little faster because she has seen the referral note from Dr. Primary that includes the penicillin allergy. She quickly confirms this with Mr. Everyman with the statement "I see from your records that you have an allergy to penicillin, is that correct?" Mr. Everyman nods, yes. She asks about other allergies to which Mr. Everyman nods, no. She notes the Penicillin Allergy in her nurse assessment. The system prompts explicitly for Latex and IV contrast. She reviews these with Mr. Everyman, who again nods no. She records "no latex allergy" "no IV contrast allergy". Dr. Eric Rescue, the physician on call in the emergency department, also reads Nurse Careful's assessment and confirms this with Mr. Everyman. Dr. Rescue decides Mr. Everyman has pneumonia and needs to be admitted. Dr. Rescue verifies the penicillin allergy in his note and prescribes Levaquin. Orders and Allergy information are sent to the hospital pharmacy system.

Post Condition: Drugs that might cause adverse reactions are avoided by the system alerts. The hospital information system has a correct and updated allergy list and this is shared with the pharmacy system. Verification that the patient does not have IV and latex allergies is recorded.

8.1.8Physician Updates Allergy Information  (REPC_ST006001UV01)

Purpose

The purpose of this storyboard is to illustrate a specialist updating allergy information and notifying Dr. Primary so appropriate updating of his system can occur.

 8.1.8.1Physician Updates Allergy Information (REPC_SN006001UV01)

Precondition: Dr. Breathright, a pulmonologist is seeing Mr. Everyman in his office after a referral from Dr. Primary. Dr. Breathright reviews the allergy information with Mr. Everyman noting the prior assessment of no known drug allergies (NKDA) from Dr. Primary. On review, Mr. Everyman recalls that he failed to mention an allergy to Penicillin. Mr. Everyman cannot recall what reaction happened, only that his mom told him as a child not to take it.

Activities: Dr. Breathright records the updated allergy to penicillin in his EMR. He includes this information in his consult note back to Dr. Primary. Alternative flows include Dr. Primary receiving message of the updated allergy information via message sent either directly, or forwarded from the central repository.

Post condition: Dr. Primary receives notification of the updated allergy information, and can update his system appropriately.

8.3.1Allergy/ intolerance request list updated  (REPC_TE000023UV01)
Description
Structured Name:  Allergyintolerance Request List Updated
Type:  User-request

A new or updated allergy list is recorded, and request is made to update another system. This notification requires confirmation of the update by the receiving system. The entire list is sent.

8.3.2Decision to not record allergy or intolerance  (REPC_TE000003UV01)
Description
Structured Name:  Allergyintolerance Event Activate Rejection
Type:  User-request

A request to record an allergy or intolerance in a patient's record has been refused. (E.g. contraindication, patient not found, etc.)

8.3.3Decision to not record allergy/ intol. change  (REPC_TE000025UV01)
Description
Structured Name:  Allergyintolerance Event Revise Rejection
Type:  User-request

A request to modify a previously-recorded allergy or intolerance has been refused (e.g. no permission, patient not found).

8.3.4Allergy and intolerances list query  (REPC_TE000006UV01)
Description
Structured Name:  Allergyintolerance List Query
Type:  User-request

Allergy/Intolerance information is requested.

8.3.5Allergy or intolerance list response  (REPC_TE000002UV01)
Description
Structured Name:  Allergyintolerance List Response
Type:  State-based

Allergy or intolerance data is returned.

8.3.6Allergy or intolerance list updated  (REPC_TE000024UV01)
Description
Structured Name:  Allergyintolerance List Updated
Type:  State-based

A new allergy list has been created, or an old allergy list has been updated. This is an unsolicited notification. The entire list and history is included.
